# Mahatma Gandhi National Foundation
The Mahatma Gandhi National Foundation is a non-profit organization established to promote the ideals and principles of Mahatma Gandhi, with a focus on social welfare, education, and community development. The foundation aims to empower marginalized communities and promote sustainable development through various initiatives and programs. It works in collaboration with government agencies, NGOs, and private sector organizations to achieve its objectives.

## Key Details
The Mahatma Gandhi National Foundation was established in 1992, and it is registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860. The foundation is headquartered in New Delhi and has a presence in several states across India. It has a governing body comprising prominent social workers, academics, and professionals who oversee its activities and programs. The foundation receives funding from government agencies, corporate donors, and individual philanthropists, and it is required to file annual returns with the Income Tax Department using Form 10B.

## Who Needs This
The Mahatma Gandhi National Foundation is relevant to NGOs, community-based organizations, and corporate companies that are involved in social welfare and community development activities. NGOs that are working in areas such as education, healthcare, and rural development can benefit from the foundation's programs and initiatives. Corporate companies that are looking to undertake corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives can also partner with the foundation to achieve their CSR objectives.

## Step-by-Step Process
To partner with the Mahatma Gandhi National Foundation, NGOs and corporate companies can follow these steps:
1. Visit the foundation's website and review its programs and initiatives.
2. Submit a proposal outlining the organization's objectives, activities, and budget.
3. The foundation's governing body will review the proposal and approve it if it aligns with the foundation's objectives.
4. The organization will be required to sign a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the foundation, outlining the terms and conditions of the partnership.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The Mahatma Gandhi National Foundation requires its partner organizations to submit annual reports and audited financial statements. The reports must be submitted within six months of the end of the financial year, and the financial statements must be audited by a chartered accountant. Failure to submit the reports and financial statements on time may result in the termination of the partnership.
