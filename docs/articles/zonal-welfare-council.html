Zonal Welfare Council

The Zonal Welfare Council is a body established under the Ministry of Labour and Employment, Government of India, to promote the welfare of workers in the Beedi, Iron, Cine, and other industries. The council operates under the Beedi and Cigar Workers (Conditions of Employment) Act, 1966, and the Cine Workers Welfare Fund Act, 1981. Its primary objective is to provide social security and welfare benefits to workers in these industries.

Key Details

The Zonal Welfare Council has nine zones across India, each responsible for implementing welfare schemes and disbursing funds to eligible workers. The council collects a welfare fund cess from employers in the Beedi and Cine industries, which is used to provide benefits such as medical assistance, education scholarships, and housing assistance to workers. The cess is collected at the rate of 1% of the wage bill for Beedi workers and 2% for Cine workers, and is deposited into the respective welfare funds.

Who Needs This

The Zonal Welfare Council is relevant to NGOs and companies operating in the Beedi, Cine, and other specified industries, particularly those with a large workforce. Employers in these industries are required to contribute to the welfare fund and comply with the provisions of the relevant Acts. NGOs working with workers in these industries may also need to interact with the Zonal Welfare Council to access benefits and services for their constituents.

Step-by-Step Process

To access benefits from the Zonal Welfare Council, workers or their employers must submit an application in the prescribed format, along with required documents such as proof of employment, age, and income. The application is then verified and processed by the council, and benefits are disbursed accordingly. The specific steps and required documents may vary depending on the type of benefit and the zone in which the worker is employed.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Employers are required to deposit the welfare fund cess within the prescribed timeline, which is typically within 30 days of the end of each quarter. Failure to comply with this requirement may result in penalties and interest on the outstanding amount. Workers or their employers must also submit applications for benefits within the specified timeframe, which may vary depending on the type of benefit.
