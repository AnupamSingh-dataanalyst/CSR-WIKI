# Skill Development CSR
Skill Development CSR refers to the corporate social responsibility initiatives undertaken by companies in India to promote vocational training and employment among youth, particularly in the unorganized sector. The primary objective of these initiatives is to enhance the employability of individuals and contribute to the country's economic growth. Under the Companies Act, 2013, companies are required to allocate a certain percentage of their profits towards CSR activities, including skill development programs.

## Key Details
The Companies Act, 2013, mandates companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, to spend at least 2% of their average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities. Skill development is one of the approved CSR activities under Schedule VII of the Act. Companies can implement skill development programs directly or through NGOs, trusts, or societies. The Ministry of Corporate Affairs has also introduced the CSR-1 form for companies to disclose their CSR expenditure, including skill development initiatives.

## Who Needs This
This applies to companies registered under the Companies Act, 2013, and having a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more. NGOs, trusts, and societies working in the skill development sector can also partner with these companies to implement CSR initiatives. Additionally, vocational training providers, educational institutions, and organizations working in the employment sector can benefit from these initiatives.

## Step-by-Step Process
To implement a skill development CSR initiative, companies can follow these steps:
1. Identify the target group and the skills required by the industry.
2. Develop a CSR policy and allocate funds for skill development programs.
3. Partner with NGOs, trusts, or societies working in the skill development sector.
4. Design and implement vocational training programs.
5. Monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of the programs.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies are required to spend the allocated CSR amount within the financial year, and any unspent amount must be transferred to the Unspent CSR Account. The deadline for filing the CSR-1 form is within 30 days from the date of the board meeting in which the CSR policy is approved. Failure to comply with the CSR provisions can result in penalties, including fines and imprisonment.
