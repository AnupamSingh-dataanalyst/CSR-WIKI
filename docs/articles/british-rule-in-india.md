# British rule in India
British rule in India, also known as the British Raj, refers to the period of British colonial rule in India that lasted from 1858 to 1947. During this time, the British government imposed its own system of laws, taxes, and administration on India, which had a profound impact on the country's economy, politics, and society. The British rule in India played a significant role in shaping the country's modern laws and institutions, including its income tax system.

## Key Details
The British rule in India began on August 2, 1858, when the British government passed the Government of India Act, which established the British Raj in India. The British Raj was characterized by a complex system of laws, including the Indian Income Tax Act of 1860, which was one of the first income tax laws in India. The British government also introduced various forms and notifications, such as the Form 16, which is still used in India today to provide certificate of tax deduction at source.
