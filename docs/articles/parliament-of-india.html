Parliament of India

The Parliament of India is the supreme legislative body of the country, consisting of the President and the two Houses: the Lok Sabha (House of the People) and the Rajya Sabha (Council of States). It is responsible for making laws, approving the government's budget, and exercising parliamentary control over the government. The Parliament of India plays a crucial role in shaping the country's laws and policies, including those related to NGOs and corporate social responsibility, such as the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010.

Key Details

The Parliament of India has 790 members, with the Lok Sabha having 543 elected members and the Rajya Sabha having 245 members, with 233 elected and 12 nominated by the President. The Lok Sabha members are elected by the people of India for a term of five years, while the Rajya Sabha members are elected by the elected members of the State Legislative Assemblies for a term of six years. The Parliament of India has the power to make laws on subjects listed in the Union List, which includes subjects such as defense, foreign affairs, and taxation.

Who Needs This

NGOs, companies, and associations that receive or intend to receive foreign contributions or hospitality need to be aware of the laws and regulations made by the Parliament of India, particularly the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010. This Act applies to all persons, including NGOs, companies, and associations, who receive or intend to receive foreign contributions or hospitality. It is essential for these entities to understand the provisions of the Act and comply with its requirements to avoid penalties and ensure smooth operation.

The deadlines and penalties for non-compliance with the laws and regulations made by the Parliament of India vary depending on the specific Act or regulation. For example, the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, requires NGOs and companies to file annual returns with the Ministry of Home Affairs by December 31st of each year.
