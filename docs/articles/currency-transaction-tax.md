# Currency Transaction Tax
The Currency Transaction Tax (CTT) is a tax levied on certain currency transactions in India, aimed at reducing the circulation of black money and promoting transparency in financial dealings. It was introduced in the Finance Act, 2004, and is governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961. The tax is applicable on transactions involving the purchase or sale of foreign currency, as well as certain domestic currency transactions.

## Key Details
The CTT is levied at a rate of 0.1% on the purchase or sale of foreign currency, and 0.05% on certain domestic currency transactions, such as the transfer of funds from a Non-Resident Indian (NRI) account to a resident account. The tax is collected by authorized banks and financial institutions, and is payable on transactions exceeding Rs. 10 lakhs in a single day. The tax is reported in the Annual Information Return (AIR) and is also subject to Tax Deduction at Source (TDS) under Section 195 of the Income-tax Act, 1961.

## Who Needs This
The CTT applies to all individuals, companies, and organizations that engage in foreign currency transactions or certain domestic currency transactions, including NRIs, foreign companies, and Indian companies with international operations. NGOs and companies that receive foreign donations or grants, or engage in international trade, are also required to comply with the CTT regulations. Additionally, banks and financial institutions that facilitate these transactions are responsible for collecting and reporting the CTT.

## Step-by-Step Process
To comply with the CTT regulations, the following steps can be taken:
* Ensure that all foreign currency transactions are properly documented and reported to the authorized banks and financial institutions.
* Verify that the tax is being collected and paid on transactions exceeding Rs. 10 lakhs in a single day.
* File the Annual Information Return (AIR) and report the CTT payments.
* Ensure that TDS is deducted and deposited on foreign currency transactions under Section 195 of the Income-tax Act, 1961.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The CTT is required to be paid on the date of the transaction, and any delay or non-payment can result in penalties and interest. The penalty for non-payment of CTT can range from Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 1 lakh, and interest can be charged at a rate of 1% per month. It is essential to comply with the CTT regulations and deadlines to avoid these penalties.
