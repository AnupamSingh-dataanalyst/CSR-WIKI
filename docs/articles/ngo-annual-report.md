# NGO Annual Report
An NGO annual report is a comprehensive document that outlines the organization's activities, achievements, and financial performance over the past year. It serves as a vital tool for transparency and accountability, providing stakeholders, including CSR donors, with a clear understanding of the NGO's work and impact. A credible annual report is essential for building trust and attracting funding from CSR donors.

## Key Details
The annual report should include key details such as the NGO's mission, objectives, and goals, as well as its programs, projects, and services. It should also provide financial information, including income statements, balance sheets, and auditor's reports, as required by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and the Companies Act, 2013. Additionally, NGOs registered under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, must submit their annual reports in Form FC-4 to the Ministry of Home Affairs.

## Who Needs This
NGOs that receive funding from CSR donors, as well as those registered under the Income-tax Act, 1961, the Companies Act, 2013, and the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, need to prepare and submit annual reports. This includes NGOs that have obtained a 12A and 80G certificate from the Income Tax Department, as well as those that have registered under the Niti Aayog's Darpan portal.

## Step-by-Step Process
To prepare an annual report, NGOs should follow these steps:
- Collect and compile data on programs, projects, and services
- Prepare financial statements, including income statements and balance sheets
- Obtain an auditor's report from a chartered accountant
- Submit the annual report to the relevant authorities, such as the Income Tax Department and the Ministry of Home Affairs
- Upload the annual report to the NGO's website and the Niti Aayog's Darpan portal

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadline for submitting the annual report varies depending on the relevant authority. For example, NGOs registered under the Income-tax Act, 1961, must submit their annual reports by September 30th of each year, while those registered under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, must submit their reports by December 31st of each year. Failure to submit the annual report on time may result in penalties, including fines and cancellation of registration.
