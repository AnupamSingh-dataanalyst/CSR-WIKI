# Registrar of Companies (India)
The Registrar of Companies (RoC) in India is a regulatory body responsible for the registration and regulation of companies in the country. It operates under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs and is responsible for ensuring that companies comply with the provisions of the Companies Act, 2013. The RoC has offices in various states and union territories, and each office has jurisdiction over companies registered within its respective region.

## Key Details
The Registrar of Companies is responsible for maintaining a registry of all companies registered in India, which includes information such as the company's name, address, directors, shareholders, and financial statements. Companies are required to file various forms and documents with the RoC, including the Memorandum and Articles of Association, Form INC-22 for verification of company's registered office, and Form AOC-4 for filing of financial statements. The RoC also issues a Certificate of Incorporation to companies after verifying their documents and information.

## Who Needs This
The Registrar of Companies is relevant to all companies registered in India, including private limited companies, public limited companies, one-person companies, and limited liability partnerships. Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) that are registered as companies under the Companies Act, 2013, also need to interact with the RoC. Additionally, foreign companies that have a presence in India are required to register with the RoC and file various documents and forms.

## Step-by-Step Process
To register a company with the RoC, the following steps need to be taken:
1. Obtain a Director Identification Number (DIN) for all directors of the company.
2. Obtain a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) for all directors of the company.
3. Reserve a unique name for the company using the MCA portal.
4. Prepare and file the Memorandum and Articles of Association with the RoC.
5. File Form INC-22 for verification of the company's registered office.
6. Obtain a Certificate of Incorporation from the RoC.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies are required to file various forms and documents with the RoC within specified deadlines. For example, Form AOC-4 for filing of financial statements needs to be filed within 30 days of the annual general meeting. Failure to file forms and documents within the specified deadlines can result in penalties, including fines and imprisonment. The RoC also has the power to strike off companies that fail to comply with the provisions of the Companies Act, 2013.
