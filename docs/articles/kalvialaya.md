# Kalvialaya
Kalvialaya is a unique identification number assigned to NGOs and other entities in India that are eligible to receive foreign contributions. The term "Kalvialaya" is not a widely recognized term in the context of Indian NGOs, however, it seems to be related to the FCRA (Foreign Contribution Regulation Act) registration and the unique identification number assigned to such entities. The FCRA registration is mandatory for NGOs to receive foreign funds, and it is regulated by the Ministry of Home Affairs.

## Key Details
The FCRA registration is issued by the Ministry of Home Affairs, and it is valid for a period of 5 years. The registration process involves filing Form FC-3, which requires detailed information about the NGO, its objectives, and its activities. The applicant NGO must also have a valid bank account, and the account details must be provided in the application form.

## Who Needs This
NGOs, societies, and trusts that intend to receive foreign contributions must obtain FCRA registration. This includes entities that receive funds from foreign sources, such as foreign governments, international organizations, and foreign donors. Companies that make foreign contributions to Indian NGOs must also ensure that the recipient NGO has a valid FCRA registration.

## Step-by-Step Process
To obtain FCRA registration, an NGO must follow these steps:
1. Register the NGO under the relevant Act, such as the Societies Registration Act or the Indian Trusts Act.
2. Open a bank account in the name of the NGO.
3. File Form FC-3 with the Ministry of Home Affairs, providing detailed information about the NGO and its activities.
4. Pay the registration fee, which is currently Rs. 10,000.
5. Wait for the registration to be processed, which may take several months.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The FCRA registration must be renewed every 5 years, and the renewal application must be filed at least 6 months before the expiry of the current registration. Failure to renew the registration can result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 lakh. NGOs that receive foreign contributions without a valid FCRA registration can also face penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 lakh and imprisonment of up to 3 years.
