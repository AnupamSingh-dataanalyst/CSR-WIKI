# Markazul Maarif
Markazul Maarif is a non-governmental organization (NGO) based in India, focused on education, research, and community development. Established with the goal of promoting social welfare and empowerment, Markazul Maarif operates under the provisions of Indian laws governing NGOs, such as the Societies Registration Act, 1860, and the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010. The organization's activities are designed to contribute to the betterment of society, aligning with the objectives of corporate social responsibility (CSR) as outlined in the Companies Act, 2013.

## Key Details
Markazul Maarif, as an NGO, is required to register under the relevant state-level Societies Registration Act, which involves filing the Memorandum and Articles of Association. For NGOs receiving foreign contributions, registration under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, is mandatory, which includes filing Form FC-3. The organization must also comply with the Income Tax Act, 1961, for tax exemptions, by applying for registration under Section 12A and 80G. Additionally, NGOs with an annual income of more than Rs. 1 crore are required to file their audited accounts with the Registrar of Societies.

## Who Needs This
Markazul Maarif's activities and compliance requirements are relevant to NGOs and companies involved in CSR initiatives in India. Specifically, this applies to:
- NGOs registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, or the Indian Trusts Act, 1882.
- Companies mandated to spend on CSR under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, looking to partner with or donate to registered NGOs.
- Foreign donors or organizations looking to contribute to Indian NGOs, which must be registered under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010.

## Step-by-Step Process
For an NGO like Markazul Maarif to be fully operational and compliant, the following steps are necessary:
1. Registration under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, or the Indian Trusts Act, 1882.
2. Applying for a PAN (Permanent Account Number) and TAN (Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number) from the Income Tax Department.
3. Registration under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, if receiving foreign funds.
4. Filing for tax exemption under Section 12A and 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961.
5. Annual filing of audited accounts and compliance reports with the relevant authorities.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
NGOs like Markazul Maarif must adhere to strict deadlines for filing returns and reports to avoid penalties. The annual income tax return must be filed by September 30th of each year. Failure to file returns or non-compliance with regulatory requirements can result in penalties, including fines and potential cancellation of registration.
