Tax Policy Center

The Tax Policy Center is a non-partisan research organization based in the United States that provides independent analysis and expertise on tax policy. It aims to inform the tax policy debate with objective, fact-based research and analysis, while also considering the long-term implications of tax policies on the economy and society. The center's work is relevant to understanding the complexities of tax policies and their impact on various sectors, including non-profit organizations and corporate social responsibility initiatives in India.

Key Details

The Tax Policy Center's work is built around several key areas, including individual and corporate taxation, tax expenditures, and international tax issues. In the context of India, understanding these concepts is crucial for NGOs and companies navigating the country's tax laws, such as the Income-tax Act, 1961, and the regulations set by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT). The center's research often involves analyzing data and trends related to tax revenues, tax rates, and the impact of tax policies on different income groups and sectors.
