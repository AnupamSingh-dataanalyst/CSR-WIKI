# Panama as a tax haven
Panama has been known as a tax haven due to its favorable tax laws and financial secrecy, attracting individuals and companies looking to minimize their tax liabilities. The country's tax regime has been a subject of controversy, with many criticizing its lack of transparency and potential for tax evasion. In recent years, Panama has faced international pressure to increase transparency and comply with global tax standards.

## Key Details
Panama's tax haven status is largely due to its low or no-tax policies on foreign-earned income, as well as its strict bank secrecy laws. The country has a territorial tax system, where only income earned within Panama is subject to taxation. Additionally, Panama has a relatively low threshold for tax exemptions, with individuals and companies only required to file tax returns if their annual income exceeds $50,000. The Panama Papers scandal in 2016 revealed widespread use of Panamanian shell companies and tax havens by individuals and companies worldwide.

## Who Needs This
This information is particularly relevant to Indian NGOs and companies with international operations or investments, as well as individuals with foreign income or assets. These entities may be subject to Indian tax laws, including the Income-tax Act, 1961, and may need to report foreign income or assets to the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT). Additionally, companies and individuals may need to comply with Indian laws and regulations related to foreign exchange, such as the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA).

Failure to comply may result in penalties, fines, or even prosecution. The CBDT may impose penalties for non-compliance, including a penalty of up to 300% of the tax owed.
