# Tax deduction
Tax deduction in India refers to the reduction in taxable income allowed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, enabling individuals and organizations to lower their tax liability. This concept is crucial for NGOs and companies as it helps them optimize their tax payments and allocate more resources to their core activities. The Income-tax Act, 1961, and the Income Tax Rules, 1962, govern the rules and regulations regarding tax deductions in India.

## Key Details
Specific sections of the Income-tax Act, 1961, such as Section 80G, provide details on tax deductions available for donations made to certain funds and institutions. For instance, donations to NGOs registered under Section 12A and 80G of the Income-tax Act, 1961, are eligible for a 50% tax deduction, subject to a maximum limit of 10% of the donor's gross total income. Forms like Form 10BD and Form 16 are used for claiming tax deductions.

## Who Needs This
NGOs, companies, and individuals who make donations to registered charitable institutions or invest in tax-saving instruments need to understand tax deductions. Specifically, NGOs registered under Section 12A and 80G of the Income-tax Act, 1961, can issue certificates to donors, enabling them to claim tax deductions. Companies can also claim tax deductions for corporate social responsibility (CSR) expenditures under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013.

## Step-by-Step Process
To claim a tax deduction, the donor or the company must obtain a receipt or certificate from the registered NGO or institution, mentioning the donation amount and the NGO's registration details. The donor or company must then file their income tax return using Form ITR-6 or Form ITR-7, as applicable, and claim the tax deduction under the relevant section of the Income-tax Act, 1961.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadline for filing income tax returns in India is typically July 31st of each year, although this may be extended in certain cases. Failure to file tax returns or claim tax deductions on time may result in penalties, such as a fine of ₹5,000 to ₹10,000 under Section 234F of the Income-tax Act, 1961.
