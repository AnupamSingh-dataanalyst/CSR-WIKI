# NGO Darpan
The NGO Darpan is a platform introduced by the NITI Aayog to create and maintain a database of all non-governmental organizations (NGOs) in India. This initiative aims to bring transparency and accountability in the functioning of NGOs, as well as to facilitate their interaction with the government and other stakeholders. By registering on the NGO Darpan portal, NGOs can access various government schemes and funding opportunities.

## Key Details
The NGO Darpan portal was launched in 2015, and it is mandatory for all NGOs to register on this platform if they want to receive grants from the government. The registration process involves filling out a simple online form, which requires basic information about the NGO, such as its name, address, objectives, and activities. NGOs can register on the portal by visiting the NITI Aayog website and clicking on the NGO Darpan link.

## Who Needs This
All NGOs in India, whether they are registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, or the Companies Act, 2013, need to register on the NGO Darpan portal. This includes NGOs working in various sectors, such as education, healthcare, environment, and social welfare. Even NGOs that are not seeking government funding are encouraged to register on the portal to take advantage of other benefits, such as access to government schemes and programs.

## Step-by-Step Process
To register on the NGO Darpan portal, NGOs need to follow these steps:
1. Visit the NITI Aayog website and click on the NGO Darpan link.
2. Fill out the online registration form, which requires basic information about the NGO.
3. Upload the required documents, such as the registration certificate, PAN card, and audited financial statements.
4. Submit the form and wait for the verification process to be completed.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
There is no specific deadline for registering on the NGO Darpan portal, but NGOs are encouraged to register as soon as possible to avoid any delays in accessing government schemes and funding opportunities. Failure to register on the portal may result in NGOs being ineligible for government grants and other benefits.
