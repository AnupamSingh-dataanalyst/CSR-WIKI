CSR-1 Form

The CSR-1 Form is a registration form for NGOs to receive Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) funds from companies in India. As per the Companies Act, 2013, and the CSR Rules, 2014, NGOs need to register with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) to become eligible for CSR funding. This form is a crucial step for NGOs to access CSR funds and work with companies on social projects.

Key Details

The CSR-1 Form is available on the MCA21 portal, and NGOs can fill it out online. The form requires details such as the NGO's name, address, registration number, PAN, and bank account information. The form also requires the NGO to declare its CSR activities, goals, and objectives. The registration is valid for a period of one year, and NGOs need to renew their registration every year.

Who Needs This

The CSR-1 Form is applicable to all NGOs that intend to receive CSR funds from companies in India. This includes Section 8 companies, societies, trusts, and other non-profit organizations. Companies that have a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, are required to spend 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities and can partner with registered NGOs.

Step-by-Step Process

To register using the CSR-1 Form, NGOs need to follow these steps: 1. Create a user account on the MCA21 portal. 2. Fill out the CSR-1 Form online and upload the required documents. 3. Pay the registration fee of Rs. 50. 4. Submit the form and wait for the registration to be approved.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The registration using the CSR-1 Form is valid for one year, and NGOs need to renew their registration every year. If an NGO fails to renew its registration, it will not be eligible to receive CSR funds. Companies that fail to spend the required 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities will have to explain the reasons for not doing so in their annual report.
