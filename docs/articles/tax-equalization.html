Tax Equalization

Tax equalization is a concept in income tax that aims to ensure fairness in taxation by adjusting the tax liability of individuals or organizations to account for differences in tax rates or laws. This concept is particularly relevant in India, where income tax is governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and is administered by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT). The goal of tax equalization is to provide a level playing field for taxpayers, regardless of their location or income source.

Key Details

Tax equalization in India involves calculating the tax liability of an individual or organization based on their worldwide income, and then adjusting it to account for taxes paid in other countries. The Income-tax Act, 1961, provides for tax equalization through provisions such as Section 90, which allows for relief from double taxation. The CBDT also issues notifications and circulars to clarify the tax equalization process, such as the Double Taxation Avoidance Agreements (DTAAs) with other countries. Forms such as Form 67 are used to claim foreign tax credit, which is an essential component of tax equalization.

Who Needs This

Tax equalization is relevant to NGOs and companies that have international operations or employees who earn income from foreign sources. This includes Indian companies with foreign subsidiaries, foreign companies with operations in India, and NGOs that receive foreign funding. Additionally, individuals who are tax residents in India but earn income from foreign sources, such as freelance workers or consultants, may also need to consider tax equalization.

Step-by-Step Process

The tax equalization process in India typically involves the following steps: 1. Calculate the worldwide income of the individual or organization. 2. Calculate the tax liability in India based on the worldwide income. 3. Calculate the taxes paid in other countries on the foreign income. 4. Claim foreign tax credit using Form 67. 5. Adjust the tax liability in India to account for the taxes paid in other countries.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The deadline for filing income tax returns in India is typically July 31st of each year, and the deadline for claiming foreign tax credit is also the same. Failure to claim foreign tax credit or to file income tax returns on time can result in penalties, such as a late filing fee of Rs. 5,000, and interest on the outstanding tax liability.
