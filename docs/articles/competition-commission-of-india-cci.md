# Competition Commission of India (CCI)
The Competition Commission of India (CCI) is a statutory body established under the Competition Act, 2002, to promote competition and prevent anti-competitive practices in India. The CCI is responsible for enforcing the provisions of the Competition Act, which aims to protect the interests of consumers and promote economic efficiency. The CCI operates under the administrative control of the Ministry of Corporate Affairs.

## Key Details
The CCI consists of a Chairperson and up to six members, appointed by the Central Government. The Commission has the power to investigate cases of anti-competitive agreements, abuse of dominant position, and combinations (mergers and acquisitions) that may have an adverse effect on competition. The CCI also has the power to impose penalties on companies found guilty of violating the Competition Act, up to 10% of the company's average turnover for the last three years. Companies with a combined turnover of over Rs. 300 crore are required to notify the CCI of any proposed combination.

## Who Needs This
The CCI's regulations apply to all companies operating in India, including NGOs and other organizations that engage in commercial activities. Companies that have a significant presence in India, either directly or through subsidiaries or joint ventures, are required to comply with the Competition Act. This includes companies that are involved in mergers and acquisitions, joint ventures, or other combinations that may have an adverse effect on competition.

## Step-by-Step Process
Companies that are required to notify the CCI of a proposed combination must submit a Form I or Form II, depending on the nature of the combination. The Form must be submitted within 30 days of the proposed combination, and must include detailed information about the companies involved, the nature of the combination, and its potential impact on competition. The CCI will then review the notification and may request additional information or conduct an investigation to determine whether the combination is likely to have an adverse effect on competition.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies that fail to notify the CCI of a proposed combination or provide false or misleading information may be subject to penalties of up to Rs. 1 crore. The CCI may also impose penalties on companies that fail to comply with its orders or directions. The CCI has 210 days to review a notification and make a decision, and companies that are required to notify the CCI must wait for the CCI's approval before proceeding with the combination.
