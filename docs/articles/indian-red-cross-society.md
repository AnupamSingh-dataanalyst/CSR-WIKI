# Indian Red Cross Society
The Indian Red Cross Society is a voluntary humanitarian organization that provides emergency assistance, disaster relief, and education to the public. Established in 1920, it is part of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement, with its headquarters in New Delhi. The society's mission is to protect human life, health, and dignity, and to promote peace and humanitarian values.

## Key Details
The Indian Red Cross Society has over 1,100 branches across the country, with more than 12 million volunteers and members. It is governed by the Indian Red Cross Society Act, 1920, and is recognized by the Government of India as a statutory body. The society's activities include blood donation, disaster management, first aid training, and health services, among others. The society is also authorized to issue the Red Cross emblem, which is protected under the Emblems and Names (Prevention of Improper Use) Act, 1950.

## Who Needs This
The Indian Red Cross Society's services and membership are open to all individuals, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), and companies in India. NGOs and companies can partner with the society to support its humanitarian activities, while individuals can become members or volunteers to contribute to the society's mission. In particular, companies operating in India can support the society's activities as part of their corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives, as mandated by the Companies Act, 2013.

## Step-by-Step Process
To become a member or volunteer of the Indian Red Cross Society, individuals can follow these steps:
1. Visit the society's website or contact the local branch to obtain a membership or volunteer application form.
2. Fill out the form and submit it along with the required documents and fees.
3. Attend an orientation or training session to learn about the society's activities and code of conduct.
4. Participate in the society's activities and events, such as blood donation camps, first aid training, and disaster relief operations.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
There are no specific deadlines or penalties for joining or supporting the Indian Red Cross Society. However, companies operating in India are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities, including support for humanitarian organizations like the Indian Red Cross Society, as per the Companies Act, 2013.
