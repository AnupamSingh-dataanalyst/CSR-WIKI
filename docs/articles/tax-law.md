# Tax Law
Tax law in India refers to the set of rules and regulations that govern the taxation of individuals and organizations in the country. The income-tax law is primarily governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, which empowers the central government to tax non-agricultural income. The law is further supplemented by the Income Tax Rules 1962, Notifications and Circulars issued by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT), annual Finance Acts, and judicial pronouncements by the Supreme and high courts of India.

## Key Details
The Income-tax Act, 1961, defines the scope of taxable income, including income from salaries, business, and investments. The Act also provides for various deductions and exemptions, such as the exemption of agricultural income as defined in Section 10(1). Key forms used in tax law include the IT Returns (ITR) forms, such as ITR-1, ITR-2, and ITR-3, which are filed through the Income Tax Department's e-filing portal. The tax slabs and rates are revised annually through the Finance Acts, with the current basic exemption limit being Rs. 2.5 lakhs for individuals.

## Who Needs This
Tax law applies to all individuals and organizations that earn income in India, including NGOs, companies, partnerships, and sole proprietorships. NGOs registered under Section 12A of the Income-tax Act, 1961, are exempt from paying income tax on their income, but are still required to file their IT Returns. Companies, on the other hand, are required to pay corporate tax on their profits, with the current tax rate being 22% for domestic companies.

## Step-by-Step Process
The step-by-step process for filing IT Returns involves registering on the Income Tax Department's e-filing portal, obtaining a PAN (Permanent Account Number), and selecting the relevant ITR form. The taxpayer must then fill in the required details, including income, deductions, and exemptions, and verify the return through an OTP sent to their registered mobile number or by signing with their digital signature.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadline for filing IT Returns is typically July 31st of each year, although this may be extended in certain cases. Failure to file IT Returns or paying tax on time can result in penalties, including a late fee of Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000, and interest on the outstanding tax amount. In severe cases, the taxpayer may also be liable for prosecution under the Income-tax Act, 1961.
