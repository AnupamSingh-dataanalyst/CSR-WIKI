# Tax Amnesty
Tax amnesty is a scheme that allows individuals and organizations to declare their previously undisclosed income and pay a specified amount of tax, without facing any penalties or prosecution. This scheme is introduced by the government to encourage taxpayers to come clean about their hidden income and bring it into the mainstream economy. The Income Tax Department in India has introduced several tax amnesty schemes over the years, aiming to increase tax compliance and reduce tax evasion.

## Key Details
The key details of tax amnesty schemes in India include the type of income that can be declared, the tax rate applicable, and the deadline for making the declaration. For example, the Income Declaration Scheme, 2016, allowed taxpayers to declare their undisclosed income and pay a tax of 45% on the declared amount. The scheme was open from June 1, 2016, to September 30, 2016, and a total of Rs 67,382 crore was declared under the scheme. Taxpayers can file Form 1 to make the declaration, and the payment can be made online through the NSDL website.

## Who Needs This
Tax amnesty schemes are applicable to all individuals and organizations that have undisclosed income, including NGOs and companies. This includes those who have not filed their income tax returns, or have filed returns but have not disclosed their entire income. NGOs and companies that have foreign income or assets are also eligible to declare their income under the tax amnesty scheme. However, the scheme is not applicable to those who are already under investigation or have been prosecuted for tax evasion.

## Step-by-Step Process
To take advantage of the tax amnesty scheme, taxpayers need to follow these steps:
1. Determine the amount of undisclosed income to be declared.
2. Calculate the tax payable on the declared amount.
3. Fill Form 1 and submit it to the Income Tax Department.
4. Make the payment of tax online through the NSDL website.
5. Receive an acknowledgement from the Income Tax Department.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadline for making the declaration under the tax amnesty scheme is usually specified by the government, and taxpayers need to make the declaration before the deadline to avoid penalties. For example, the deadline for the Income Declaration Scheme, 2016, was September 30, 2016. If taxpayers fail to make the declaration before the deadline, they may face penalties, including a fine of up to Rs 10 lakh, and prosecution under the Income Tax Act, 1961.
