# Disable Welfare Trust of India
The Disable Welfare Trust of India is a non-governmental organization dedicated to improving the lives of people with disabilities in India. The trust works towards providing education, employment, and healthcare opportunities to individuals with disabilities, promoting their overall well-being and empowerment. By supporting the trust, individuals and organizations can contribute to creating a more inclusive and equitable society for people with disabilities.

## Key Details
The Disable Welfare Trust of India is registered under the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, and is eligible to receive tax-deductible donations under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961. The trust has a unique registration number and is required to file annual returns with the Income Tax Department using Form ITR-7. The trust's objectives include providing financial assistance, educational support, and medical aid to people with disabilities, with a focus on promoting their economic and social independence.

## Who Needs This
The Disable Welfare Trust of India is relevant to non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and companies that work with people with disabilities or aim to support disability-related causes. NGOs registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, or the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, can partner with the trust to amplify their impact. Companies operating in India can also support the trust as part of their corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives, as mandated by Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, and the Companies (Corporate Social Responsibility Policy) Rules, 2014.

## Step-by-Step Process
To support the Disable Welfare Trust of India, individuals and organizations can follow these steps:
1. Visit the trust's website or office to learn more about its objectives and activities.
2. Fill out the donation form, which can be downloaded from the trust's website or obtained from its office.
3. Submit the completed form along with the donation amount, which can be made online or offline.
4. Claim tax deduction under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961, by obtaining a receipt from the trust.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Donations to the Disable Welfare Trust of India are eligible for tax deduction under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961, and must be made before the end of the financial year (March 31st) to claim the deduction in the same year. Failure to file annual returns with the Income Tax Department using Form ITR-7 may result in penalties and interest on the trust.
