Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad

The Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad is a national-level organization in India that aims to promote and regulate non-formal education, vocational training, and skill development programs. It was established to provide an alternative education system, focusing on practical skills and knowledge that can help individuals become self-sufficient and contribute to the country's economic growth. The organization operates under the aegis of the Government of India and is recognized by various state governments and educational institutions.

Key Details

The Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad offers various courses and programs in fields such as agriculture, healthcare, engineering, and management, among others. It has a network of over 1,000 study centers and training institutions across India, providing education and training to more than 500,000 students every year. The organization is headquartered in New Delhi and has regional offices in major cities like Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai. To establish a study center or training institution under the Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad, an applicant must submit Form BLS-1, along with a fee of Rs. 10,000, and meet certain infrastructure and faculty requirements.

Who Needs This

The Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad is relevant to non-governmental organizations (NGOs), community-based organizations, and private institutions that provide education and training services, particularly in rural and underserved areas. Companies and corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives that focus on education and skill development can also partner with the Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad to support its programs and activities. Additionally, individuals who want to pursue non-formal education or vocational training can benefit from the organization's courses and programs.

Step-by-Step Process

To establish a study center or training institution under the Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad, the following steps can be followed: 1. Submit an application in Form BLS-1, along with the required fee and documents. 2. Meet the infrastructure and faculty requirements specified by the organization. 3. Undergo an inspection and evaluation by the Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad's team. 4. Receive approval and certification from the organization. 5. Comply with the organization's norms and standards for education and training.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The Bharat Lok Shiksha Parishad has specific deadlines for submitting applications, fees, and documents. For example, the application for establishing a study center or training institution must be submitted at least 6 months before the proposed date of commencement. Failure to comply with the organization's norms and standards can result in penalties, including cancellation of certification and reputation damage.
