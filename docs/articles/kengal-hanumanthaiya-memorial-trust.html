Kengal Hanumanthaiya Memorial Trust

The Kengal Hanumanthaiya Memorial Trust is a public charitable trust established in memory of Kengal Hanumanthaiya, the former Chief Minister of Karnataka. The trust aims to promote education, healthcare, and social welfare in the state of Karnataka. It provides financial assistance to deserving students, supports healthcare initiatives, and undertakes various social welfare programs.

Key Details

The Kengal Hanumanthaiya Memorial Trust is registered under the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, and is exempt from income tax under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961. The trust has a corpus fund of over ₹5 crores and disburses around ₹1 crore annually to support its various programs. The trust is managed by a board of trustees, which includes prominent personalities from the fields of education, healthcare, and social work.

Who Needs This

The Kengal Hanumanthaiya Memorial Trust is relevant to NGOs, educational institutions, and healthcare organizations operating in the state of Karnataka. These organizations can apply for financial assistance from the trust to support their programs and initiatives. Additionally, individuals who wish to contribute to the trust's activities can donate to the trust, which is eligible for tax exemption under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961.

To apply for financial assistance from the Kengal Hanumanthaiya Memorial Trust, NGOs and organizations need to follow these steps: 1. Submit a proposal outlining their program or initiative, including its objectives, budget, and expected outcomes. 2. The Kengal Hanumanthaiya Memorial Trust typically invites applications for financial assistance between June and August of each year. Applications received after the deadline may not be considered.
