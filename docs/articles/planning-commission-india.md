# Planning Commission (India)
The Planning Commission of India was a government institution that played a crucial role in formulating India's Five-Year Plans, which aimed to promote economic growth and development. Established in 1950, the Planning Commission was responsible for assessing the country's resources, identifying priorities, and allocating funds for various sectors. Although it was replaced by the NITI Aayog in 2015, understanding the Planning Commission's role and functions is essential for grasping India's economic development trajectory.

## Key Details
The Planning Commission was headed by the Prime Minister of India, with a Deputy Chairman and other members appointed by the government. The Commission's main objective was to promote rapid economic growth and improve the standard of living of the people. It formulated 12 Five-Year Plans, with the first plan launched in 1951 and the last one concluding in 2017. The Planning Commission also played a key role in allocating funds to various sectors, including healthcare, education, and infrastructure.

## Who Needs This
NGOs and companies involved in economic development, policy-making, and public-private partnerships may need to understand the role and functions of the Planning Commission. This includes organizations working in areas such as poverty alleviation, education, healthcare, and infrastructure development. Additionally, companies seeking government funding or partnerships for their projects may need to familiarize themselves with the Planning Commission's policies and procedures.

## Step-by-Step Process
Although the Planning Commission is no longer in operation, its functions have been taken over by the NITI Aayog. To engage with the NITI Aayog, organizations can follow these steps:
- Visit the NITI Aayog website to learn about its initiatives and priorities
- Register on the NITI Aayog portal to access relevant information and resources
- Submit proposals or applications for funding or partnerships, as applicable

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
As the Planning Commission is no longer operational, there are no specific deadlines or penalties associated with it. However, organizations engaging with the NITI Aayog should be aware of the timelines and guidelines for submitting proposals or applications, as well as the evaluation criteria and selection processes.
