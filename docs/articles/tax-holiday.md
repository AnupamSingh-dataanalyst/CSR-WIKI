# Tax holiday
A tax holiday is a period during which certain taxes are not imposed or are reduced, providing relief to taxpayers. In the context of income tax in India, a tax holiday refers to a specific provision under the Income-tax Act, 1961, that exempts certain incomes or activities from taxation for a specified period. This provision is often used to promote economic growth, investment, and development in specific sectors or regions.

## Key Details
The tax holiday provision in India is governed by Section 80-I of the Income-tax Act, 1961, which provides a deduction of 100% of the profits derived from certain industrial undertakings, such as those engaged in infrastructure development, housing projects, or information technology. The tax holiday is available for a period of 10 years, with the option to claim the deduction for a block of 10 years out of 15 years, starting from the year in which the undertaking begins to operate. The tax holiday is subject to certain conditions, such as the undertaking being located in a specified area, having a minimum investment, and meeting certain other criteria.

## Who Needs This
The tax holiday provision applies to companies and undertakings engaged in specified industries or activities, such as infrastructure development, housing projects, information technology, and biotechnology. Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and charitable institutions may also be eligible for tax exemptions under Section 11 of the Income-tax Act, 1961, if they meet certain conditions and file Form 10 in the prescribed manner.

## Step-by-Step Process
To claim the tax holiday, the undertaking must file Form 10CCB with the Income-tax Department, along with the required documents, such as the audit report, balance sheet, and profit and loss account. The undertaking must also meet the conditions specified in Section 80-I and the Income-tax Rules, 1962.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The tax holiday is available for a specified period, and the undertaking must claim the deduction within the prescribed time limit. Failure to meet the conditions or file the required forms may result in denial of the tax holiday or imposition of penalties under the Income-tax Act, 1961.
