# Tax Farming
Tax farming is a system where the government leases out the right to collect taxes to a private entity or individual, known as a tax farmer, for a fixed period of time. This system was historically used in various countries, including India, to collect taxes such as land revenue, customs duty, and other levies. However, with the introduction of modern taxation systems, tax farming has largely been abolished in India.

## Key Details
The concept of tax farming is not directly applicable to the current income tax system in India, which is governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and other related laws. However, some key details related to tax collection in India include the requirement for taxpayers to file their income tax returns using Form ITR-1 to Form ITR-7, depending on their income and category. The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) issues notifications and circulars to clarify tax laws and procedures, and the Finance Acts amend the tax laws every year.

## Who Needs This
Tax farming is not a relevant concept for NGOs or companies in India, as the current tax system is based on self-assessment and direct collection by the government. However, understanding the history and evolution of tax systems can be useful for organizations involved in advocacy, policy research, or historical studies. NGOs and companies may need to comply with current tax laws and regulations, such as filing tax returns, paying taxes, and maintaining accounting records.

## Step-by-Step Process
There is no step-by-step process for tax farming in India, as it is not a current practice. However, taxpayers, including NGOs and companies, need to follow the steps outlined in the Income-tax Act, 1961, and related laws to comply with tax regulations, such as:
* Maintaining accounting records
* Filing tax returns
* Paying taxes
* Responding to tax notices

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
While tax farming is not relevant, taxpayers in India need to be aware of important deadlines and penalties related to tax compliance, such as:
* Filing tax returns by July 31st of each year
* Paying advance taxes by June 15th, September 15th, December 15th, and March 15th
* Penalties for late filing or non-payment of taxes, which can range from 1% to 12% per month
