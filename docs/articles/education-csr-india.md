# Education CSR India
Education CSR in India refers to the corporate social responsibility initiatives undertaken by companies to promote education and improve the quality of learning in the country. These initiatives can range from building and supporting schools, providing scholarships to underprivileged students, to promoting digital literacy among various communities. The Companies Act, 2013, mandates companies to spend a certain percentage of their profits on CSR activities, including education.

## Key Details
The Companies Act, 2013, requires companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, to spend at least 2% of their average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities, including education. Companies can undertake education-related CSR activities through their own foundations, trusts, or societies, or by collaborating with other NGOs and organizations. The CSR policy of a company must be disclosed on its website and in its annual report on Form MGT-9.

## Who Needs This
This applies to all companies registered in India, including private and public limited companies, that meet the threshold criteria specified in the Companies Act, 2013. NGOs and education institutions can also benefit from this by partnering with companies to implement education-related CSR projects. Additionally, companies can also collaborate with the government to support initiatives such as the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan and the Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan.

## Step-by-Step Process
To undertake education-related CSR activities, companies can follow these steps:
1. Identify the education-related CSR projects or activities they want to undertake.
2. Formulate a CSR policy that includes education as one of the focus areas.
3. Establish a CSR committee to oversee the implementation of the CSR policy.
4. Collaborate with NGOs, education institutions, or government agencies to implement the CSR projects.
5. Monitor and evaluate the impact of the CSR projects and disclose the details in their annual report.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies are required to spend the mandated 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities, including education, within the financial year. If a company fails to spend the required amount, it must disclose the reasons for not doing so in its annual report on Form MGT-9. The company may also be liable to pay a penalty of up to Rs. 1 crore for non-compliance.
