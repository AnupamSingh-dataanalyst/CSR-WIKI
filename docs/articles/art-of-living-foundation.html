Art of Living Foundation

The Art of Living Foundation is a non-profit, educational, and humanitarian organization founded by Sri Ravi Shankar in 1981. It is headquartered in Bengaluru, India, and has centers in over 150 countries worldwide. The foundation aims to promote individual well-being, societal development, and global harmony through various programs and initiatives.

Key Details

The Art of Living Foundation is registered under the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, and is exempt from income tax under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961. It has a unique PAN number and is required to file its annual returns with the Income Tax Department. The foundation's flagship program is the Art of Living course, which has been attended by over 30 million people worldwide.

Who Needs This

The Art of Living Foundation's programs and initiatives are open to individuals from all walks of life, including those from non-governmental organizations (NGOs), corporate social responsibility (CSR) teams, and educational institutions. NGOs and companies can partner with the foundation to implement its programs and initiatives, such as the Art of Living course, yoga and meditation programs, and disaster relief efforts.

Step-by-Step Process

To partner with the Art of Living Foundation, NGOs and companies can follow these steps: 1. Visit the foundation's website and fill out the partnership inquiry form. 2. Discuss the proposed partnership with the foundation's representatives. 3. Sign a memorandum of understanding (MoU) outlining the terms and conditions of the partnership. 4. Implement the agreed-upon programs and initiatives.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The Art of Living Foundation is required to file its annual returns with the Income Tax Department by September 30th of each year. Failure to do so may result in penalties and fines. Additionally, the foundation must comply with the provisions of the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, and file its returns with the Ministry of Home Affairs by December 31st of each year.
