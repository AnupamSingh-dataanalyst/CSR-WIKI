Creative Services Support Group

The Creative Services Support Group is a concept that refers to a collective of professionals or organizations that provide creative services, such as graphic design, writing, and digital media production, to support the marketing and communication efforts of non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives in India. This group can be a valuable resource for NGOs and companies looking to amplify their social impact through effective storytelling and visual communication. By leveraging the expertise of creative professionals, NGOs and companies can enhance their brand identity, engage their target audience, and ultimately drive social change.

Key Details

The Creative Services Support Group can take various forms, including freelancers, agencies, or in-house teams, and can provide a range of services such as branding, website design, social media management, and content creation. In India, NGOs and companies can avail of these services to comply with the CSR provisions under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, and to report their CSR activities on the Ministry of Corporate Affairs' portal, MCA21. The CSR policy of a company must specify the activities to be undertaken, the amount to be spent, and the modalities of execution, which can include engaging creative services to support their CSR initiatives.

Who Needs This

NGOs and companies in India that are required to spend a certain percentage of their net profit on CSR activities, as per the Companies Act, 2013, can benefit from the Creative Services Support Group. Specifically, companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, are required to constitute a CSR committee and formulate a CSR policy. These companies can engage creative services to support their CSR initiatives, such as creating awareness campaigns, designing marketing materials, and producing digital content.

Step-by-Step Process

To engage a Creative Services Support Group, NGOs and companies can follow these steps: 1. Define their CSR objectives and identify the creative services required to support their initiatives. 2. Research and shortlist potential creative service providers, such as freelancers, agencies, or in-house teams. 3. Evaluate the portfolio, expertise, and experience of the shortlisted providers. 4. Engage the selected provider through a formal agreement, such as a contract or a memorandum of understanding. 5. Collaborate with the provider to develop and implement the creative services, such as branding, website design, or content creation.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies are required to spend the prescribed amount on CSR activities within the financial year, and any unspent amount must be transferred to a CSR fund or a registered trust. The CSR committee must also disclose the CSR policy and the activities undertaken in the company's annual report, which must be filed with the Registrar of Companies within 30 days of the annual general meeting. Failure to comply with the CSR provisions can result in penalties, such as a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore.
