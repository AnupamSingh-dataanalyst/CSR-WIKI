# Indian National Congress (R)
The Indian National Congress (R) refers to a faction of the Indian National Congress that emerged in 1969, led by Indira Gandhi. This faction was also known as the Congress (Requisitionist) or simply Congress (R), and it played a significant role in Indian politics during the 1970s. The "R" in the name stood for "Requisitionist," indicating that this faction was formed in response to a requisition by Indira Gandhi to the Congress party.

## Key Details
The Indian National Congress (R) was formed after a split in the Indian National Congress in 1969, with Indira Gandhi as its leader. The party's symbol was a cow and a calf, and it won a significant number of seats in the 1971 general elections. The party's formation was a result of a power struggle within the Congress party, with Indira Gandhi emerging as the dominant leader.
