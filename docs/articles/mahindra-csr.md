# Mahindra CSR
The Mahindra Group, a leading Indian conglomerate, undertakes its corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives through the Mahindra Foundation, which focuses on promoting education, healthcare, and environmental sustainability. The Mahindra Foundation works closely with various non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and stakeholders to implement its CSR projects. With a strong commitment to giving back to society, the Mahindra Group has been actively engaged in CSR activities for several decades.

## Key Details
The Mahindra Group's CSR initiatives are guided by the Companies Act, 2013, which mandates that companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, allocate at least 2% of their average net profits for the preceding three financial years towards CSR activities. The Mahindra Foundation implements its CSR projects in accordance with the Mahindra Group's CSR policy, which is approved by the company's Board of Directors. The foundation's CSR initiatives are implemented through various programs, including the Mahindra Pride Schools, the Mahindra Hariyali initiative, and the Mahindra Scholastic Awards.

## Who Needs This
The Mahindra Group's CSR initiatives are relevant to NGOs and companies that operate in the areas of education, healthcare, and environmental sustainability. NGOs that are registered under the Income Tax Act, 1961, and have a valid 12A and 80G certificate, are eligible to partner with the Mahindra Foundation to implement CSR projects. Companies that are looking to undertake CSR activities in accordance with the Companies Act, 2013, can also benefit from the Mahindra Group's CSR initiatives.

## Step-by-Step Process
To partner with the Mahindra Foundation, NGOs can follow these steps:
1. Register on the Mahindra Foundation's website and submit a proposal outlining their CSR project.
2. The proposal will be reviewed by the Mahindra Foundation's CSR team, which will assess the project's alignment with the foundation's CSR objectives.
3. If the proposal is approved, the NGO will be required to sign a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the Mahindra Foundation, outlining the terms and conditions of the partnership.
4. The NGO will then be required to implement the CSR project and submit regular progress reports to the Mahindra Foundation.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The Mahindra Group's CSR initiatives are subject to the deadlines and penalties outlined in the Companies Act, 2013. Companies that fail to allocate the required amount towards CSR activities will be required to explain the reasons for the shortfall in their annual report. Additionally, companies that fail to comply with the CSR provisions of the Companies Act, 2013, may be subject to penalties, including fines and imprisonment.
