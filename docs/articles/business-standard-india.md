# Business Standard India
Business Standard India is a leading Indian business newspaper that provides comprehensive coverage of the country's business and economy. It is a trusted source of news, analysis, and data for businesses, policymakers, and individuals interested in the Indian economy. The newspaper is particularly relevant for NGOs and companies operating in India, as it provides valuable insights into the country's regulatory environment, including laws such as the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010.

## Key Details
The Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, is a consolidating act that regulates the acceptance and utilization of foreign contribution or foreign hospitality by certain individuals or associations or companies. The act prohibits the acceptance and utilization of foreign contribution or foreign hospitality for any activities detrimental to the national interest. Key details of the act include the requirement for NGOs and companies to register with the Ministry of Home Affairs and obtain a Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act (FCRA) registration number, which is valid for five years. The act also mandates the filing of annual returns in Form FC-4, which must be submitted within nine months of the end of the financial year.

## Who Needs This
The Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, applies to all NGOs and companies in India that receive or intend to receive foreign contributions. This includes associations, companies, and individuals who receive foreign hospitality or foreign funding for various purposes, such as charitable, educational, or social activities. NGOs and companies that are exempt from the provisions of the act include those that are exempt under Section 6(1) of the act, such as government companies and entities established by the government.

## Step-by-Step Process
To register under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, NGOs and companies must follow a step-by-step process, which includes:
* Submitting an application in Form FC-3 to the Ministry of Home Affairs
* Providing required documents, such as a certificate of registration, memorandum of association, and audited financial statements
* Paying the required registration fee, which is currently Rs. 7,500
* Obtaining an FCRA registration number, which is valid for five years

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
NGOs and companies that are required to register under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, must do so within the specified timeframe to avoid penalties. The act mandates that annual returns in Form FC-4 must be filed within nine months of the end of the financial year. Failure to file annual returns or non-compliance with the provisions of the act can result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 lakh and imprisonment for up to one year.
