# Government of India
The Government of India is the federal government of India, responsible for the welfare and governance of its citizens. It is divided into three branches: the legislative, executive, and judiciary, with the President of India serving as the head of state and the Prime Minister as the head of government. The government plays a crucial role in regulating and supporting non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives in the country.

## Key Details
The Government of India has established various ministries and departments to oversee different aspects of governance, including the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, which is responsible for administering the Companies Act 2013, the Companies Act 1956, the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008, and the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016. The government has also introduced various forms and portals, such as the MCA21 portal, to facilitate online filing and registration of companies. Additionally, the government has set thresholds for CSR spending, requiring companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, to spend at least 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities.

## Who Needs This
This information is relevant to NGOs and companies operating in India, particularly those that are required to register with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs or comply with CSR regulations. This includes companies with a significant presence in India, as well as NGOs that receive funding from Indian companies or the government. Foreign companies operating in India may also need to comply with Indian laws and regulations.

## Step-by-Step Process
To register a company in India, the following steps can be taken: 
1. Obtain a Director Identification Number (DIN) from the Ministry of Corporate Affairs.
2. Reserve a unique name for the company using the MCA21 portal.
3. File the incorporation documents, including the Memorandum and Articles of Association, with the Registrar of Companies.
4. Obtain a certificate of incorporation from the Registrar of Companies.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies are required to file their annual returns and financial statements with the Registrar of Companies within 30 days of the annual general meeting. Failure to comply with CSR regulations can result in penalties, including fines and imprisonment. The government has also introduced penalties for non-compliance with other regulations, such as the Companies Act 2013.
