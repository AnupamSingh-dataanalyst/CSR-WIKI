Manipal Foundation

The Manipal Foundation is a non-profit organization established by the Manipal Group, a well-known business conglomerate in India. The foundation focuses on supporting various social causes, including education, healthcare, and community development, with the aim of making a positive impact on society. Through its initiatives, the Manipal Foundation collaborates with NGOs, government agencies, and other stakeholders to address pressing social issues.

Key Details

The Manipal Foundation was established in 2004, with its headquarters in Manipal, Karnataka. The foundation has supported over 100 NGOs and community organizations across India, providing financial assistance, technical expertise, and capacity-building programs. To be eligible for support, NGOs must be registered under the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, or the Societies Registration Act, 1860, and have a valid 12A and 80G certification from the Income Tax Department.

Who Needs This

The Manipal Foundation's support is available to NGOs and community organizations working in the areas of education, healthcare, and community development. Companies operating in India can also partner with the Manipal Foundation to fulfill their Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) obligations under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013. Specifically, companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities.

Step-by-Step Process

To apply for support from the Manipal Foundation, NGOs and community organizations can follow these steps: 1. Submit an expression of interest outlining their project proposal, budget, and expected outcomes. 2. Fill out the application form, which can be downloaded from the Manipal Foundation's website. 3. Attach required documents, including registration certificates, audited financial statements, and a detailed project plan. 4. Submit the application to the Manipal Foundation's office, either by email or post.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The Manipal Foundation typically accepts applications on a rolling basis, but NGOs and community organizations are advised to submit their applications at least 6 months prior to the proposed project start date. Failure to comply with the foundation's reporting and monitoring requirements may result in termination of support and recovery of funds.
