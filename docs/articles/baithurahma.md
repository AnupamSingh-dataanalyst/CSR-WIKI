# Baithurahma
Baithurahma is a charitable trust in Kerala, India, known for its philanthropic activities and community service. The trust was established with the aim of providing assistance to the underprivileged and promoting social welfare. Baithurahma is registered under the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, and is exempt from income tax under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961.

## Key Details
Baithurahma has been actively involved in various charitable activities, including providing financial assistance to poor patients, distributing food and clothing to the needy, and supporting education for underprivileged children. The trust has a governing body that oversees its activities and ensures that its objectives are met. Baithurahma is required to file its annual income tax return in Form ITR-7 and is also required to file its annual report with the Registrar of Trusts.

## Who Needs This
Baithurahma's services are available to all individuals and organizations in need, regardless of their background or location. However, the trust's activities are primarily focused on the state of Kerala, and its services are mostly utilized by NGOs, charitable institutions, and underprivileged individuals in the region. Companies and organizations that are involved in corporate social responsibility (CSR) activities may also partner with Baithurahma to support its charitable initiatives.

## Step-by-Step Process
To avail of Baithurahma's services, individuals or organizations can follow these steps:
1. Contact Baithurahma's office to inquire about the types of services offered and the eligibility criteria.
2. Submit an application form, which can be obtained from the trust's office or downloaded from its website.
3. Provide required documents, such as proof of income, identity, and residence.
4. Wait for the application to be processed and approved by the trust's governing body.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Baithurahma is required to file its annual income tax return by September 30th of each year. Failure to file the return on time may result in a penalty of up to Rs. 1,00,000. The trust is also required to file its annual report with the Registrar of Trusts within six months of the end of the financial year.
