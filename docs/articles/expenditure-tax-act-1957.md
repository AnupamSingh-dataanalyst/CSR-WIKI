# Expenditure Tax Act, 1957
The Expenditure Tax Act, 1957 is a law in India that imposes a tax on excessive expenditure by individuals, with the aim of reducing inequality and promoting social welfare. This act is part of the broader income tax framework in India, which is governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961 and other related laws. The Expenditure Tax Act, 1957 was enacted to curb conspicuous consumption and generate revenue for the government.

## Key Details
The Expenditure Tax Act, 1957 applies to individuals with a certain level of expenditure, which is defined as expenditure exceeding Rs. 50,000 in a year. The tax rate under this act ranges from 10% to 30% of the excess expenditure, depending on the amount of expenditure. The act requires individuals to file a return of their expenditure in Form ET, which is to be submitted to the Assessing Officer.

## Who Needs This
The Expenditure Tax Act, 1957 applies to individuals who have an annual expenditure exceeding Rs. 50,000, including non-resident Indians and foreign nationals who have spent more than 120 days in India in a financial year. This act is particularly relevant to high-net-worth individuals, business owners, and those with significant disposable income. NGOs and companies may also need to consider this act if they provide excessive benefits or perquisites to their employees or directors.

## Step-by-Step Process
To comply with the Expenditure Tax Act, 1957, individuals need to maintain a record of their expenditure throughout the year, including receipts, invoices, and bank statements. They must then file Form ET with the Assessing Officer by the due date, which is typically July 31st of each year. The form requires details of the individual's expenditure, income, and tax liability.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The due date for filing Form ET is July 31st of each year, and failure to file the return or pay the tax on time can result in penalties and interest. The penalty for late filing can range from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000, and interest is charged at the rate of 1% per month on the outstanding tax amount.
