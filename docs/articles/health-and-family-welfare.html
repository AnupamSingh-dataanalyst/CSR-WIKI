Health and Family Welfare

Health and Family Welfare is a critical sector in India that focuses on improving the health and well-being of its citizens, with a special emphasis on family welfare initiatives. The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare is the primary government agency responsible for implementing policies and programs in this sector. The health and family welfare sector is also closely linked with corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives, as mandated by the Companies Act 2013, which requires companies to spend a certain percentage of their profits on social welfare activities, including health and family welfare.

Key Details

The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare has launched several key initiatives, including the National Health Mission (NHM), which aims to provide universal access to equitable, affordable, and quality health services. The NHM has a budget allocation of over Rs. 30,000 crores for the current financial year. Companies can claim CSR exemptions for spending on health and family welfare initiatives, such as setting up hospitals, healthcare facilities, and sanitation facilities, by filing Form CSR-1 with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs.

Who Needs This

NGOs and companies involved in health and family welfare initiatives, including those working on sanitation, hygiene, and healthcare programs, need to be aware of the guidelines and regulations outlined by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare. Companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crores or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crores or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crores or more, are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities, including health and family welfare initiatives.

Step-by-Step Process

To claim CSR exemptions for health and family welfare initiatives, companies need to follow a step-by-step process: (1) identify a health and family welfare project or initiative, (2) ensure that the project aligns with the Schedule VII of the Companies Act 2013, (3) file Form CSR-1 with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, and (4) maintain detailed records of the CSR spending, including receipts, invoices, and project reports.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies are required to spend the mandated 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities, including health and family welfare initiatives, within the financial year. Failure to comply with the CSR regulations can result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 25 lakhs and imprisonment of up to three years.
