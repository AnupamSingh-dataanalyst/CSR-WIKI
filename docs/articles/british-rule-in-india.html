British rule in India

British rule in India, also known as the British Raj, refers to the period of British colonial rule in India that lasted from 1858 to 1947. During this time, the British government imposed its own system of laws, taxes, and administration on India, which had a profound impact on the country's economy, politics, and society. The British rule in India played a significant role in shaping the country's modern laws and institutions, including its income tax system.

Key Details

The British rule in India began on August 2, 1858, when the British government passed the Government of India Act, which established the British Raj in India.
