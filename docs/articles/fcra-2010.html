Laws & Regulations

FCRA 2010

The Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010 is an act of the Parliament of India that regulates the acceptance and utilisation of foreign contribution or foreign hospitality by certain individuals or associations or companies. This act aims to prohibit the acceptance and utilisation of foreign contribution or foreign hospitality for any activities detrimental to the national interest. It was designed to address the shortcomings of its predecessor law enacted in 1976 and provides a consolidated framework for the regulation of foreign contributions in India.

Key Details

The FCRA 2010 requires individuals or associations to register with the Central Government if they wish to accept foreign contributions. The registration is granted for a period of 5 years, and the applicant must submit Form FC-3 along with the required documents and a fee of Rs. 7,500 for a new registration or Rs. 3,000 for a renewal. The applicant must also have a valid bank account and a PAN number.

Who Needs This

This act applies to all NGOs, associations, and companies that receive foreign contributions, including donations, grants, or any other form of financial assistance from foreign sources. It also applies to individuals who receive foreign hospitality, such as travel or accommodation expenses paid by a foreign source. However, certain entities are exempt from the provisions of the FCRA 2010, such as government agencies, diplomatic missions, and international organisations.

Step-by-Step Process

To register under the FCRA 2010, an applicant must follow these steps: 1. Submit an online application in Form FC-3 on the FCRA website. 2. Upload the required documents, including the organisation's constitution, PAN number, and bank account details. 3. Pay the registration fee of Rs. 7,500 for a new registration or Rs. 3,000 for a renewal. 4. Wait for the approval of the application, which may take several months.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The registration under the FCRA 2010 is valid for 5 years, and the applicant must renew it before the expiry date to continue receiving foreign contributions. Failure to register or renew the registration can result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 lakh and imprisonment for up to 3 years. The applicant must also file an annual return in Form FC-4 within 9 months of the end of the financial year.
