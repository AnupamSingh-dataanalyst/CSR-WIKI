Liechtenstein tax affair

The Liechtenstein tax affair, also known as the LGT affair, refers to a major tax scandal that came to light in 2008, involving the Liechtenstein Global Trust (LGT) bank and numerous individuals who had secretly deposited funds in the bank to evade taxes. This scandal led to a global crackdown on tax evasion and the implementation of stricter tax regulations. In India, the scandal prompted the government to take measures to prevent tax evasion and money laundering.

Key Details

The Liechtenstein tax affair involved over 1,000 individuals from around the world, including India, who had deposited approximately $5 billion in the LGT bank. The scandal was exposed when a former LGT employee stole confidential data and sold it to the German government, which then shared the information with other countries, including India. The Indian government used this information to identify and prosecute individuals who had evaded taxes.

This information is relevant to NGOs and companies in India that have international transactions or dealings, as well as individuals who have foreign bank accounts or assets. It is particularly important for those who have not disclosed their foreign assets or income in their tax returns, as they may be liable for penalties and prosecution.
