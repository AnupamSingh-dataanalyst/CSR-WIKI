# India Photo Archive Foundation
The India Photo Archive Foundation is a non-profit organization dedicated to preserving and showcasing India's photographic heritage. Established to promote the understanding and appreciation of photography as an art form and a medium for social commentary, the foundation works towards creating a comprehensive archive of photographs that reflect the country's history, culture, and diversity. Through its efforts, the foundation aims to make this valuable resource accessible to researchers, scholars, and the general public.

## Key Details
The India Photo Archive Foundation was founded in 2007 by noted photographer and curator, Aditya Arya, with the objective of collecting, preserving, and exhibiting photographs that document India's history and development. The foundation has a collection of over 50,000 photographs, including rare and historic images from the 19th and 20th centuries. To achieve its goals, the foundation collaborates with museums, galleries, and other cultural institutions, and offers fellowships and grants to researchers and photographers working on projects related to Indian photography.
