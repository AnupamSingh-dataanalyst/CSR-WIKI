CSR Proposal Writing India

Writing a winning CSR grant proposal is crucial for Indian NGOs and social enterprises to secure funding from companies for their social and environmental projects. A well-crafted CSR proposal helps companies achieve their Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) objectives under the Companies Act, 2013, while also supporting meaningful social causes. By following a structured approach, NGOs can increase their chances of securing CSR funding from Indian companies.

Key Details

To write a winning CSR proposal, it is essential to understand the key details of the CSR policy in India. Under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, companies with a net worth of ₹500 crore or more, or a turnover of ₹1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of ₹5 crore or more, are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities. The CSR proposal should be submitted to the company's CSR committee, which reviews and approves the proposal. The proposal should include details of the project, such as the objective, scope, timeline, budget, and expected outcomes, and should be submitted in the prescribed format, which may vary from company to company.

Who Needs This

This guide is applicable to Indian NGOs, social enterprises, and companies that are required to spend on CSR activities under the Companies Act, 2013. NGOs and social enterprises can use this guide to write a winning CSR proposal and secure funding from companies, while companies can use this guide to understand the key details of the CSR policy and to review and approve CSR proposals. Specifically, this guide is relevant to companies that are required to file Form CSR-1 with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, and to NGOs that are registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, or under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, or the Public Trusts Act, 1950.

Step-by-Step Process

To write a winning CSR proposal, follow these steps: 1. Research the company's CSR policy and objectives to ensure alignment with the proposal. 2. Define the project objective, scope, timeline, and budget. 3. Conduct a needs assessment and baseline study to demonstrate the project's impact. 4. Develop a detailed project plan, including milestones and expected outcomes. 5. Prepare a comprehensive budget, including all costs and expenses. 6. Submit the proposal to the company's CSR committee, along with all required documents and information.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities during each financial year, and to disclose their CSR policy and spending in their annual report. If a company fails to spend the required amount on CSR activities, it is required to explain the reasons for the shortfall in its annual report, and to transfer any unspent amount to a CSR fund. The deadline for spending on CSR activities is the end of each financial year, i.e., March 31st.
