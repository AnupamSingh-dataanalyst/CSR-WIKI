# Taxation
Taxation in India refers to the process of levying taxes on individuals and organizations by the government, with income tax being a significant component. The income-tax law in India is governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and related rules, notifications, and circulars. The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) plays a crucial role in administering and implementing the income-tax law.

## Key Details
The Income-tax Act, 1961, defines the scope of taxable income, including exemptions and deductions. For example, agricultural income is exempt from income tax under Section 10(1) of the Act. The tax rates and slabs are revised annually through the Finance Acts. Key forms used for income tax purposes include the ITR-1 (Sahaj) for individuals, ITR-4 (Sugam) for presumptive income, and ITR-6 for companies. The tax threshold for individuals is Rs. 2.5 lakhs per annum, with tax rates ranging from 5% to 30% depending on the income slab.

## Who Needs This
All individuals and organizations earning income in India are required to comply with the income-tax law. This includes NGOs, companies, partnerships, and sole proprietorships. Specifically, NGOs registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, or under the Income-tax Act, 1961, as charitable trusts or societies, are required to file income tax returns and comply with tax laws. Companies, including those engaged in corporate social responsibility (CSR) activities, must also comply with tax laws and file returns using the applicable ITR forms.

## Step-by-Step Process
The step-by-step process for income tax compliance involves:
1. Obtaining a Permanent Account Number (PAN) from the Income Tax Department.
2. Filing income tax returns using the applicable ITR form.
3. Paying advance tax or self-assessment tax, if applicable.
4. Responding to notices or queries from the Income Tax Department.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadline for filing income tax returns is typically July 31st of each year, with some exceptions for companies and other entities. Late filing of returns may attract penalties, including a fee of Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000, depending on the delay. Additionally, failure to pay advance tax or self-assessment tax may result in interest and penalty charges.
