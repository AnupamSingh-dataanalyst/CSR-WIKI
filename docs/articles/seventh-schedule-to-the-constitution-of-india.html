Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India

The Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India is a crucial component of the Indian Constitution that outlines the division of powers between the Union government and the state governments. It comprises three lists: the Union List, the State List, and the Concurrent List, which enumerate the subjects on which the Union and state governments can legislate. The Seventh Schedule plays a vital role in governing various aspects of Indian law, including income tax, which is governed by Entry 82 of the Union List.

Key Details

The Seventh Schedule consists of 97 subjects in the Union List, 66 subjects in the State List, and 47 subjects in the Concurrent List. Entry 82 of the Union List empowers the central government to tax non-agricultural income, while agricultural income is defined in Section 10(1) of the Income-tax Act, 1961. The income-tax law in India comprises the Income-tax Act, 1961, Income Tax Rules 1962, Notifications and Circulars issued by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT), annual Finance Acts, and judicial pronouncements by the Supreme and high courts of India.

Who Needs This

The Seventh Schedule applies to all entities operating in India, including NGOs, companies, and individuals, as it governs the division of powers between the Union government and state governments. NGOs and companies involved in activities related to income tax, such as taxation, accounting, and finance, need to be aware of the provisions of the Seventh Schedule, particularly Entry 82 of the Union List. Additionally, state governments and local authorities need to understand the subjects listed in the State List and the Concurrent List to exercise their legislative powers effectively.
