Ministry of Home Affairs

The Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) is a government ministry of India, responsible for the maintenance of internal security and domestic policy. The MHA plays a crucial role in regulating the acceptance and utilization of foreign contributions by non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and other entities in India. The MHA is also responsible for implementing the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010 (FCRA), which aims to regulate foreign contributions and hospitality in India.

Key Details

The Ministry of Home Affairs is responsible for administering the FCRA, which requires NGOs and other entities to register with the MHA before accepting foreign contributions. The FCRA applies to all associations, companies, and individuals who intend to accept foreign contributions, with some exceptions. Key details include: * Registration fee: Rs. 7,500 for new registrations and Rs. 5,000 for renewal of registration * Renewal period: 5 years * Forms: FC-1, FC-2, FC-3, FC-4, and FC-6 * Online portal: FCRA online services, available on the MHA website

Who Needs This

The FCRA applies to all NGOs, associations, and companies that intend to accept foreign contributions, including: * Voluntary associations * Charitable trusts * Societies * Companies * Educational institutions * Research institutions * Hospitals and healthcare organizations

Step-by-Step Process

To register under the FCRA, NGOs and other entities need to follow these steps: 1. Submit an online application on the FCRA online services portal 2. Fill out Form FC-1 and upload required documents 3. Pay the registration fee 4. Wait for verification and approval by the MHA

Important Deadlines / Penalties

  • Registration renewal: 6 months before the expiry of the current registration
  • Annual return filing: 31st December of each year
  • Penalty for non-compliance: Up to 5 years imprisonment and/or fine up to Rs. 1 lakh
