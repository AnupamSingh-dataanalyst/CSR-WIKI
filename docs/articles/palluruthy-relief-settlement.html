Palluruthy Relief Settlement

The Palluruthy Relief Settlement is a rehabilitation project initiated by the Government of Kerala to provide relief and rehabilitation to families affected by the construction of the Cochin Port and other development projects in the region. Established in 1956, the settlement aims to provide a comprehensive package of support services, including housing, education, healthcare, and livelihood opportunities, to the displaced families. The settlement is a notable example of a government-led rehabilitation effort in India, demonstrating a commitment to social welfare and community development.

Key Details

The Palluruthy Relief Settlement covers an area of approximately 120 acres, with over 1,500 families benefiting from the project. The settlement provides a range of amenities, including schools, healthcare centers, community halls, and markets. The project is implemented by the Kerala Government's Revenue Department, in collaboration with other government agencies and non-governmental organizations. The settlement is funded through a combination of government allocations and donations from private organizations.

Who Needs This

The Palluruthy Relief Settlement is primarily intended for families displaced by the construction of the Cochin Port and other development projects in the region. Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and community-based organizations working with displaced communities may also benefit from the settlement's services and amenities. Companies operating in the region, particularly those involved in infrastructure development and construction, may also be required to contribute to the settlement's funds as part of their corporate social responsibility (CSR) obligations, as mandated by the Companies Act, 2013, and the CSR Rules, 2014.

Step-by-Step Process

The process of availing benefits from the Palluruthy Relief Settlement typically involves the following steps: 1. Identification of eligible families by the Revenue Department 2. Verification of documents, including proof of displacement and residency 3. Allocation of housing and other amenities 4. Provision of support services, including education, healthcare, and livelihood training

Important Deadlines / Penalties

There are no specific deadlines or penalties associated with the Palluruthy Relief Settlement, as it is an ongoing government project. However, NGOs and companies contributing to the settlement's funds must comply with the relevant regulations and timelines, including filing annual reports with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs through the MCA21 portal.
