# Tax Justice Network
The Tax Justice Network is an independent organization that works to promote tax justice and fairness globally, with a focus on addressing issues related to tax evasion, avoidance, and corruption. It aims to create a more equitable and transparent tax system, ensuring that governments have the resources needed to provide essential public services. The network collaborates with various stakeholders, including governments, civil society organizations, and individuals, to achieve its objectives.

## Key Details
The Tax Justice Network was founded in 2003 and is headquartered in the United Kingdom. It has a global presence, with partners and members from over 100 countries. The network's work is guided by several key principles, including the need for transparency, accountability, and fairness in tax systems. In India, the Tax Justice Network's work is relevant in the context of the country's income tax laws, which are governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and related rules, notifications, and circulars issued by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT).

## Who Needs This
The Tax Justice Network's work is relevant to various stakeholders, including non-governmental organizations (NGOs), companies, and individuals who are interested in promoting tax justice and fairness. In India, NGOs and companies that are required to comply with the country's income tax laws, including those related to tax deduction at source (TDS) and tax collected at source (TCS), may find the network's resources and guidance useful. Additionally, individuals who are concerned about tax evasion and avoidance, and who want to promote a more equitable and transparent tax system, may also be interested in the Tax Justice Network's work.

While there is no specific step-by-step process for engaging with the Tax Justice Network, organizations and individuals who are interested in promoting tax justice and fairness can take several steps, including: staying informed about tax-related issues and developments, participating in advocacy campaigns and initiatives, and supporting organizations that work on tax justice issues. In India, there are several important deadlines and penalties related to income tax compliance, including the deadline for filing tax returns (July 31st for individuals and September 30th for companies), and the penalty for late filing of tax returns (up to Rs. 10,000). NGOs and companies that fail to comply with the country's income tax laws may also face other penalties, including fines and interest on unpaid taxes.
