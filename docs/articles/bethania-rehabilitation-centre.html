Bethania Rehabilitation Centre

The Bethania Rehabilitation Centre is a non-profit organization that provides rehabilitation services to people with disabilities, aiming to empower them to lead independent lives. Established with a mission to promote inclusivity and accessibility, the centre offers a range of programs and facilities tailored to the needs of its beneficiaries. By fostering an environment of care, support, and empowerment, the Bethania Rehabilitation Centre strives to make a positive impact on the lives of individuals with disabilities.

Key Details

The Bethania Rehabilitation Centre operates under the provisions of the Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, and is registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860. The centre provides services such as physiotherapy, occupational therapy, and vocational training, with a focus on enabling individuals with disabilities to acquire skills and gain employment. The centre's operations are guided by a governing body that oversees its management and ensures compliance with relevant laws and regulations, including the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, and the Income-tax Act, 1961.

Who Needs This

The Bethania Rehabilitation Centre's services are primarily targeted at non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and companies that work with people with disabilities, or those that aim to promote disability rights and inclusivity. NGOs registered under Section 12A of the Income-tax Act, 1961, and companies that have a corporate social responsibility (CSR) policy in place, as mandated by Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, may find the centre's services relevant to their objectives. Additionally, organizations that receive foreign contributions and are registered under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, may also benefit from the centre's expertise.

The Bethania Rehabilitation Centre is required to comply with various regulatory deadlines, including the submission of annual reports to the Registrar of Societies and the filing of income-tax returns.
