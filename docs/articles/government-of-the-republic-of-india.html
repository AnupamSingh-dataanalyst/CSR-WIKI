Government of the Republic of India

The Government of the Republic of India is the central government of India, responsible for the welfare and development of its citizens. It is a federal parliamentary democratic republic, consisting of the executive, legislative, and judicial branches. The government is headed by the Prime Minister, who is the head of government, while the President serves as the head of state.

Key Details

The Government of India has a complex structure, with 29 states and 8 union territories. The NITI Aayog, established in 2015, serves as the apex public policy think tank, replacing the Planning Commission of India. The government has various ministries, such as the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, which oversees corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives, and the Ministry of Finance, which regulates taxation and financial matters.

Who Needs This

This information is relevant to non-governmental organizations (NGOs), companies, and individuals who interact with the government or receive funding from it. Specifically, NGOs that receive foreign funding must comply with the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, while companies must adhere to the Companies Act, 2013, and CSR regulations. State governments and local authorities also need to understand the government's structure and policies to implement development projects and schemes.

Step-by-Step Process

To engage with the Government of India, NGOs and companies can follow these steps: 1. Register with the relevant ministry or department, such as the Ministry of Corporate Affairs or the Ministry of Home Affairs. 2. Obtain necessary licenses and permits, like the FCRA registration for foreign-funded NGOs. 3. Comply with applicable laws and regulations, such as the CSR rules under the Companies Act, 2013. 4. Submit reports and returns, like the annual FCRA return or the CSR report.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

NGOs and companies must meet deadlines for submitting reports and returns to avoid penalties. For example, the deadline for filing the FCRA annual return is December 31st of each year, while the CSR report must be filed with the annual financial statements. Failure to comply with regulations can result in fines, penalties, or even cancellation of registration.
