# Tax revenue to GDP ratio
The tax revenue to GDP ratio is a measure of a country's tax revenue as a percentage of its Gross Domestic Product (GDP). It indicates the proportion of a country's economic output that is collected as taxes by the government. In the context of India, this ratio is crucial in understanding the country's tax structure and its impact on the economy.

## Key Details
The tax revenue to GDP ratio in India has been steadily increasing over the years, with a significant rise after the implementation of the Goods and Services Tax (GST) in 2017. As per the data from the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT), the tax revenue to GDP ratio in India was around 11.4% in the financial year 2020-21. The income-tax law in India, which includes the Income-tax Act, 1961, and the Income Tax Rules, 1962, plays a significant role in determining this ratio.

## Who Needs This
This concept is relevant to all NGOs and companies operating in India, as it affects their tax liabilities and compliance requirements. Specifically, it applies to:
* NGOs registered under the Income-tax Act, 1961, and claiming tax exemptions under Section 12A or 80G
* Companies incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, and required to pay corporate tax
* Individuals with taxable income, including those working with NGOs or companies

## Step-by-Step Process
While there is no specific step-by-step process for calculating the tax revenue to GDP ratio, NGOs and companies can follow these general steps to understand their tax liabilities and compliance requirements:
* Register with the Income Tax Department and obtain a Permanent Account Number (PAN)
* File annual income tax returns using Form ITR-6 (for companies) or Form ITR-7 (for NGOs)
* Comply with tax deduction and collection requirements, such as TDS and TCS

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadlines for filing income tax returns and paying taxes in India are as follows:
* July 31st of each year for individuals and NGOs
* October 31st of each year for companies
* Penalties for late filing or non-payment of taxes can range from 1% to 12% per month, depending on the type of tax and the duration of delay
