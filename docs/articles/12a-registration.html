12A Registration

12A registration is a crucial step for non-governmental organizations (NGOs) in India to obtain income tax exemption under the Income Tax Act, 1961. This registration allows NGOs to claim exemption from paying income tax on their surplus, enabling them to utilize their resources more efficiently for their social causes. By obtaining 12A registration, NGOs can also enhance their credibility and attract more donors.

Key Details

To be eligible for 12A registration, an NGO must be registered under one of the following Acts: the Societies Registration Act, 1860, the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, or the Companies Act, 2013 (as a Section 8 company). The application for 12A registration is made in Form 10A, which is filed online through the Income Tax Department's portal. The registration is usually granted for a period of 5 years, after which it needs to be renewed.

Who Needs This

12A registration is applicable to all NGOs that are established in India and are engaged in charitable activities, such as education, healthcare, and poverty alleviation. This includes trusts, societies, and Section 8 companies that are registered in India. Foreign NGOs operating in India may also be eligible for 12A registration, provided they meet the necessary conditions.

Step-by-Step Process

To obtain 12A registration, an NGO needs to follow these steps: 1. Ensure that the NGO is registered under one of the eligible Acts. 2. Prepare the necessary documents, including the registration certificate, trust deed or memorandum of association, and audited financial statements. 3. Fill out Form 10A online through the Income Tax Department's portal. 4. Upload the required documents and pay the application fee. 5. Wait for the processing of the application, which may take several months.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The application for 12A registration should be made within one year from the date of establishment of the NGO. If the application is not made within this timeframe, the NGO may be required to pay income tax on its surplus. Failure to renew the 12A registration within the specified period may also result in the cancellation of the registration.
