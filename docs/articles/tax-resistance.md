# Tax Resistance
Tax resistance refers to the practice of refusing to pay taxes as a form of protest or dissent, often used to challenge government policies or laws. In the context of Indian taxation, tax resistance can take various forms, including non-payment of income tax, withholding of taxes, or other forms of non-compliance. The Indian government has established a robust tax system, with the Income-tax Act, 1961, and related rules and regulations governing the taxation of non-agricultural income.

## Key Details
The Income-tax Act, 1961, is the primary legislation governing income tax in India, with Section 10(1) defining agricultural income as exempt from taxation. The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) issues notifications, circulars, and guidelines to clarify tax laws and procedures. Taxpayers are required to file their income tax returns using forms such as ITR-1, ITR-2, or ITR-3, depending on their income and category. The tax rates and slabs are revised annually through the Finance Acts, with the current tax slabs ranging from 5% to 30% for different income levels.

## Who Needs This
Tax resistance is not a common or recommended practice for NGOs or companies in India, as it can result in severe penalties and legal consequences. However, certain individuals or organizations may consider tax resistance as a form of protest or to highlight social or economic issues. This may include individuals or groups advocating for tax reforms, social justice, or environmental causes. Companies or NGOs with international operations or investments may also need to consider tax resistance in other countries, depending on their global tax obligations.

## Step-by-Step Process
There is no specific step-by-step process for tax resistance in India, as it is not a recognized or legitimate means of tax compliance. Taxpayers are expected to comply with tax laws and regulations, and any form of non-compliance can result in penalties, fines, or even prosecution. Instead, taxpayers can explore legitimate avenues for tax planning, exemptions, or deductions, such as claiming charitable donations or investing in tax-saving instruments.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Taxpayers in India are required to file their income tax returns by July 31st of each year, with some exceptions for certain categories of taxpayers. Failure to file tax returns or pay taxes on time can result in penalties, interest, and fines, ranging from 1% to 12% of the tax liability per month. In severe cases, tax evasion or non-compliance can lead to prosecution under the Income-tax Act, 1961, with penalties including imprisonment and fines.
