# ITC CSR
ITC Limited's Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives are a significant part of the company's commitment to giving back to society. ITC's CSR efforts focus on various areas, including rural development, women empowerment, and environmental sustainability, with notable initiatives such as e-Choupal and watershed development. Through these initiatives, ITC aims to make a positive impact on the lives of people and the environment.

## Key Details
ITC's CSR initiatives are implemented through the ITC Foundation, which oversees the company's social and environmental initiatives. The e-Choupal initiative, launched in 2000, aims to empower farmers by providing them with access to information, markets, and services. ITC's watershed development program, launched in 2000, has covered over 1.5 million acres of land, benefiting thousands of farmers. The company's women empowerment initiatives include the establishment of self-help groups and vocational training programs.

## Who Needs This
ITC's CSR initiatives are relevant to NGOs and companies working in the areas of rural development, women empowerment, and environmental sustainability. NGOs can partner with ITC to implement CSR projects, while companies can learn from ITC's CSR initiatives and incorporate similar practices into their own businesses. Additionally, companies looking to comply with the CSR provisions under the Companies Act, 2013, can draw inspiration from ITC's CSR efforts.

## Step-by-Step Process
To partner with ITC on CSR initiatives, NGOs can follow these steps:
1. Identify areas of alignment between the NGO's objectives and ITC's CSR focus areas.
2. Submit a proposal outlining the project's objectives, scope, and budget.
3. ITC's CSR team will review the proposal and assess its feasibility and potential impact.
4. If approved, the NGO will work with ITC to implement the project, with regular monitoring and evaluation.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
While there are no specific deadlines for partnering with ITC on CSR initiatives, NGOs and companies should be aware of the CSR provisions under the Companies Act, 2013, which require companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, to spend at least 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities. Failure to comply with these provisions can result in penalties.
