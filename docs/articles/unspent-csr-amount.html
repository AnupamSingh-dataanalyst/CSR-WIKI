CSR Concepts

Unspent CSR Amount

The Unspent CSR Amount refers to the remaining balance of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) funds that companies are required to spend on social welfare activities, as mandated by the Companies Act, 2013. If a company fails to utilize its entire CSR budget for a given financial year, the unspent amount must be transferred to a designated fund. This provision ensures that CSR funds are utilized effectively and efficiently.

Key Details

The Companies Act, 2013, and the CSR Rules, 2014, govern the treatment of unspent CSR amounts. According to Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, any unspent CSR amount must be transferred to a Special Account opened by the company for CSR activities, or to the PM CARES Fund, or to a fund specified in Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013. The transfer must be made within six months of the end of the financial year. Form CSR-2 is used to report the transfer of unspent CSR amounts to the PM CARES Fund or to a fund specified in Schedule VII.

Who Needs This

This provision applies to all companies that are required to undertake CSR activities under the Companies Act, 2013. These companies include those with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more. NGOs that partner with these companies to implement CSR projects should also be aware of the rules regarding unspent CSR amounts.

Step-by-Step Process

To transfer unspent CSR amounts, companies must follow these steps: 1. Calculate the unspent CSR amount at the end of the financial year. 2. Open a Special Account for CSR activities, if not already done. 3. Transfer the unspent CSR amount to the Special Account, the PM CARES Fund, or a fund specified in Schedule VII within six months of the end of the financial year. 4. File Form CSR-2 with the Registrar of Companies to report the transfer.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies must transfer unspent CSR amounts within six months of the end of the financial year. Failure to do so may result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore. The company may also be required to pay interest on the unspent amount.
