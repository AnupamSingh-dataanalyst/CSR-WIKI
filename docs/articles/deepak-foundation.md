# Deepak Foundation
The Deepak Foundation is a non-profit organization established in 1980 by Deepak Nitrite Limited, a leading chemical manufacturing company in India. The foundation aims to improve the quality of life of marginalized communities through various initiatives in education, healthcare, and sustainable livelihoods. With a strong focus on community development, the Deepak Foundation has been working towards creating a positive impact on the lives of people in need.

## Key Details
The Deepak Foundation has implemented various programs and projects across India, benefiting over 100,000 people annually. The foundation's key initiatives include providing educational support to underprivileged children, promoting healthcare services in rural areas, and empowering women through vocational training and economic empowerment programs. The foundation also focuses on environmental sustainability, with initiatives such as tree plantation drives and waste management programs.

## Who Needs This
The Deepak Foundation's initiatives are primarily targeted towards marginalized communities, including underprivileged children, women, and rural populations. Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and corporate social responsibility (CSR) teams of companies can collaborate with the Deepak Foundation to support its initiatives and contribute to the betterment of society. Companies can also fulfill their CSR obligations under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, by supporting the foundation's projects.

## Step-by-Step Process
To collaborate with the Deepak Foundation, NGOs and companies can follow these steps:
1. Identify the area of collaboration, such as education, healthcare, or sustainable livelihoods.
2. Review the foundation's initiatives and projects to determine the best fit for collaboration.
3. Establish contact with the foundation's team to discuss potential partnership opportunities.
4. Submit a proposal outlining the scope of work, budget, and expected outcomes.
5. Finalize the partnership agreement and begin implementation of the collaborative project.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
As the Deepak Foundation is a non-profit organization, it is exempt from income tax under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961. However, the foundation is required to file its annual returns with the Income Tax Department and comply with other regulatory requirements. Companies collaborating with the foundation must also ensure compliance with CSR regulations under the Companies Act, 2013, and file their CSR reports with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs through the MCA21 portal.
