# Haji Public School
Haji Public School is a charitable institution providing educational services to underprivileged students in India. Established with the goal of promoting education and social welfare, the school operates under the provisions of Indian laws governing charitable trusts and societies. The school's mission is to provide quality education and empower disadvantaged communities.

## Key Details
The Haji Public School is registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, and is exempt from income tax under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961. The school has a governing body consisting of seven members, with a term of three years. The school's annual budget is approximately ₹50 lakhs, which is utilized for educational expenses, infrastructure development, and staff salaries.

## Who Needs This
Haji Public School is relevant to non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and companies operating in the education sector, particularly those focused on charitable activities and social welfare. NGOs and companies seeking to collaborate with or support the school must comply with Indian laws and regulations governing charitable donations, tax exemptions, and foreign contributions. This includes filing Form FC-1 with the Ministry of Home Affairs for foreign contributions and obtaining a certificate under Section 12A of the Income Tax Act, 1961, for tax exemption.

## Step-by-Step Process
To collaborate with or support Haji Public School, NGOs and companies must follow these steps:
1. Register with the relevant authorities, such as the Ministry of Corporate Affairs for companies or the State Registrar for NGOs.
2. Obtain necessary certifications, including an FCRA (Foreign Contribution Regulation Act) certificate for foreign contributions.
3. File annual returns with the Income Tax Department and the Ministry of Home Affairs, as applicable.
4. Comply with provisions of the Companies Act, 2013, and the Income Tax Act, 1961, regarding charitable donations and tax exemptions.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
NGOs and companies supporting Haji Public School must adhere to the following deadlines:
- File annual returns with the Income Tax Department by September 30th of each year.
- Submit FCRA returns to the Ministry of Home Affairs by December 31st of each year.
Failure to comply with these deadlines may result in penalties, including fines and cancellation of tax exemption certificates.
