# Gurukul Pratishthan
Gurukul Pratishthan is a non-governmental organization (NGO) based in India that focuses on providing education and skill development to underprivileged children and youth. The organization aims to empower individuals through quality education, vocational training, and community development programs. By doing so, Gurukul Pratishthan strives to create a positive impact on the lives of marginalized communities and contribute to the country's overall social and economic growth.

## Key Details
Gurukul Pratishthan operates in accordance with the provisions of the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, and is registered under Section 12A of the Income-tax Act, 1961. The organization is also eligible to receive foreign contributions under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, and is registered with the National Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) Hub. To receive donations and grants, Gurukul Pratishthan is required to file annual returns with the Income Tax Department using Form ITR-7 and maintain audited financial statements.

## Who Needs This
Gurukul Pratishthan's programs and services are designed to benefit underprivileged children and youth, particularly those from economically disadvantaged backgrounds. NGOs and companies that focus on education, skill development, and community development may find it beneficial to partner with or support Gurukul Pratishthan. Additionally, corporate entities looking to fulfill their CSR obligations under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, may consider collaborating with Gurukul Pratishthan to implement CSR projects.

## Step-by-Step Process
To partner with or support Gurukul Pratishthan, interested NGOs and companies can follow these steps:
1. Visit the organization's website to learn more about their programs and services.
2. Contact Gurukul Pratishthan's management team to discuss potential collaboration opportunities.
3. Review and sign a memorandum of understanding (MoU) or partnership agreement outlining the terms and conditions of the collaboration.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Gurukul Pratishthan is required to file its annual returns and audited financial statements with the Income Tax Department by September 30th of each year. Failure to comply with these requirements may result in penalties, fines, or even cancellation of the organization's registration.
