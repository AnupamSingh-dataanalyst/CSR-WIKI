Tax exemption

Tax exemption in India refers to the exemption of certain individuals, companies, or organizations from paying income tax on their income. This exemption is granted under the Income-tax Act, 1961, and is subject to various conditions and limitations. The main purpose of tax exemption is to promote certain activities or organizations that are considered beneficial to society, such as charitable institutions or non-governmental organizations (NGOs).

Key Details

To be eligible for tax exemption, NGOs and other organizations must meet certain conditions, such as being registered under Section 12A of the Income-tax Act, 1961, and filing Form 10G with the Income Tax Department. The exemption is available under Section 11 of the Act, which allows exemption of income from property held for charitable or religious purposes. The exemption limit for NGOs is Rs. 1 lakh to Rs. 10 lakhs, depending on the type of organization and its activities. Additionally, NGOs must file their annual income tax returns in Form ITR-7 and audited accounts in Form 10B.

Who Needs This

Tax exemption is applicable to various types of NGOs and companies in India, including charitable trusts, societies, and section 8 companies. These organizations must be registered under the relevant laws, such as the Societies Registration Act, 1860, or the Indian Trusts Act, 1882. Companies that are engaged in charitable or social activities, such as education, healthcare, or environmental conservation, may also be eligible for tax exemption.

Step-by-Step Process

To apply for tax exemption, NGOs and companies must follow these steps: 1. Register the organization under the relevant laws, such as the Societies Registration Act, 1860, or the Indian Trusts Act, 1882. 2. Apply for registration under Section 12A of the Income-tax Act, 1961, by filing Form 10A. 3. File Form 10G with the Income Tax Department to claim exemption under Section 11 of the Act. 4. File annual income tax returns in Form ITR-7 and audited accounts in Form 10B.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The deadline for filing income tax returns for NGOs and companies is September 30th of each year. Failure to file returns or non-compliance with the conditions of tax exemption can result in penalties, such as cancellation of registration or imposition of tax liability. NGOs and companies must also maintain proper accounts and records to support their claim for tax exemption.
