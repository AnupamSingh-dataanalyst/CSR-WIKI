# Nambikkai
Nambikkai is a Tamil word that translates to "faith" or "trust" in English, but in the context of Indian corporate social responsibility (CSR), it refers to a unique identification number assigned to NGOs and other entities that work with companies to implement CSR projects. The Nambikkai portal is an online platform where NGOs can register and obtain this unique identification number, which is mandatory for NGOs to collaborate with companies on CSR initiatives. The portal aims to bring transparency and accountability to CSR projects in India.

## Key Details
The Nambikkai portal is managed by the Indian government, and NGOs can register on the portal by providing basic information such as their name, address, and PAN number. The registration process is free, and NGOs can obtain their unique identification number within a few days of registration. The Nambikkai portal also allows companies to search for registered NGOs and track the progress of CSR projects.

## Who Needs This
Nambikkai is mandatory for all NGOs that work with companies on CSR projects in India. This includes NGOs that receive funding from companies for social welfare projects, as well as those that partner with companies to implement CSR initiatives. Companies that spend more than Rs. 50 lakhs on CSR projects in a financial year are also required to use the Nambikkai portal to track their CSR spending and report on their CSR activities.

## Step-by-Step Process
To register on the Nambikkai portal, NGOs need to follow these steps:
1. Visit the Nambikkai portal website and click on the "Register" button.
2. Fill in the registration form with basic information such as name, address, and PAN number.
3. Upload required documents such as registration certificate, PAN card, and audited financial statements.
4. Verify email address and mobile number.
5. Obtain unique identification number after verification.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies that fail to spend at least 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities in a financial year can face penalties under the Companies Act, 2013. NGOs that fail to register on the Nambikkai portal can also face difficulties in receiving funding from companies for CSR projects.
