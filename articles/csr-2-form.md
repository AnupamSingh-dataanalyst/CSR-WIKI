# CSR-2 Form
The CSR-2 Form is a crucial document that companies in India must file annually with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) to report their Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) activities. This form is a mandatory requirement under the Companies Act, 2013, and is used to track and monitor the CSR spending of companies. The information provided in the CSR-2 Form helps the government to assess the impact and effectiveness of CSR initiatives.

## Key Details
The CSR-2 Form is filed on the MCA21 portal, and companies are required to provide detailed information about their CSR policy, activities, and expenditures. The form requires companies to disclose their CSR budget, the amount spent on CSR activities, and the details of the CSR projects implemented during the financial year. Companies must also attach a certificate from a Chartered Accountant or a Cost Accountant, certifying that the CSR expenditure has been incurred in accordance with the CSR policy of the company and the provisions of the Companies Act, 2013.

## Who Needs This
The CSR-2 Form is applicable to companies that are required to spend on CSR activities under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013. These companies include those with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more. This applies to both Indian companies and foreign companies having a branch or project office in India. NGOs and other organizations that receive CSR funding from companies are also indirectly affected by this form, as they need to ensure that the companies they partner with are compliant with the CSR regulations.

## Step-by-Step Process
To file the CSR-2 Form, companies need to follow these steps:
1. Prepare the necessary documents, including the CSR policy, CSR budget, and details of CSR projects.
2. Obtain a certificate from a Chartered Accountant or a Cost Accountant, certifying the CSR expenditure.
3. Log in to the MCA21 portal and fill out the CSR-2 Form.
4. Attach the required documents, including the certificate from the Chartered Accountant or Cost Accountant.
5. Submit the form and pay the applicable fees.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The CSR-2 Form must be filed within 30 days of the end of the financial year, i.e., by April 30th of each year. Companies that fail to file the CSR-2 Form on time may be subject to penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 lakh and imprisonment of up to 3 years.
