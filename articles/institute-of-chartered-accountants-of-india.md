# Institute of Chartered Accountants of India
The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) is a statutory body established under the Chartered Accountants Act, 1949, to regulate the profession of chartered accountancy in India. The ICAI is responsible for setting standards, conducting examinations, and granting certificates to chartered accountants. It also plays a crucial role in ensuring that companies and NGOs in India comply with financial regulations and maintain transparency in their accounting practices.

## Key Details
The ICAI has over 300,000 members and operates through 164 branches and 34 chapters overseas. It is headquartered in New Delhi and has five regional councils. The ICAI conducts the Chartered Accountancy (CA) examination, which consists of three levels: Foundation, Intermediate, and Final. The examination is held twice a year, in May and November. To become a member of the ICAI, individuals must fill out Form 18 and pay a registration fee, which currently stands at Rs. 600 for students and Rs. 200 for members.

## Who Needs This
The ICAI's services and regulations apply to all companies and NGOs in India that require auditing and accounting services. This includes companies registered under the Companies Act, 2013, and NGOs registered under the Income Tax Act, 1961, and the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010. Specifically, companies with a turnover of over Rs. 1 crore and NGOs with an annual income of over Rs. 50 lakh are required to have their accounts audited by a chartered accountant.

## Step-by-Step Process
To become a chartered accountant, individuals must follow these steps:
1. Register with the ICAI through Form 18.
2. Complete the CA Foundation course and examination.
3. Complete the CA Intermediate course and examination.
4. Complete the CA Final course and examination.
5. Gain practical experience through articleship training.
6. Apply for membership through Form 2.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The ICAI has strict deadlines for exam registrations, which typically fall in February and August for the May and November exams, respectively. Late registration is allowed with a penalty fee of Rs. 600. Failure to comply with ICAI regulations can result in penalties, including fines and suspension of membership.
