Arghyam

Arghyam is a Bangalore-based philanthropic organization that works to address the issue of water scarcity in India. Founded in 2005 by Rohini Nilekani, Arghyam aims to support initiatives that improve access to clean water and sanitation, particularly in rural areas. Through its grants and partnerships, Arghyam seeks to promote sustainable water management practices and strengthen the capacity of local communities to manage their water resources.

Key Details

Arghyam provides grants to non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and community-based organizations working on water and sanitation issues in India. The organization focuses on supporting initiatives that promote sustainable water management, improve access to clean water and sanitation, and enhance the livelihoods of rural communities. Arghyam's grants range from Rs. 5 lakhs to Rs. 50 lakhs, and the organization typically supports projects that have a duration of 1-3 years.

Who Needs This

Arghyam's grants and support are available to NGOs and community-based organizations working on water and sanitation issues in India. This includes organizations that focus on issues such as watershed management, rural water supply, sanitation, and hygiene. Companies and corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives that focus on water conservation and sanitation may also partner with Arghyam to support its initiatives.

Step-by-Step Process

To apply for a grant from Arghyam, NGOs and community-based organizations must submit a proposal outlining their project's objectives, methodology, and budget. The proposal must be submitted through Arghyam's online portal, and must include detailed information about the organization, its experience, and its capacity to implement the project. Arghyam's team reviews the proposals and selects the organizations that will receive grants.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Arghyam typically announces its grant cycle twice a year, and the deadlines for submitting proposals are usually in June and December. Organizations that receive grants from Arghyam must submit regular progress reports and undergo periodic evaluations to ensure that they are meeting their project's objectives.
