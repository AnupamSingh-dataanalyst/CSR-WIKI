Make in India

The Make in India initiative is a flagship program launched by the Government of India to promote domestic manufacturing and attract foreign investment. Launched in 2014, the program aims to transform India into a global manufacturing hub by creating a favorable business environment and providing incentives to companies. The program is overseen by the NITI Aayog, the apex public policy think tank of the Government of India.

Key Details

The Make in India initiative covers 25 sectors, including automotive, aviation, biotechnology, and construction. The program offers various incentives, such as tax exemptions, subsidies, and investment promotions, to companies that set up manufacturing units in India. Companies can register for the program through the Department of Industrial Policy and Promotion (DIPP) website and fill out Form MSI-1 to provide details about their project.

Who Needs This

The Make in India initiative applies to companies, including NGOs and social enterprises, that want to set up manufacturing units in India. This includes domestic companies, foreign companies, and joint ventures. The program is particularly relevant for companies in the 25 sectors covered by the initiative, as well as for companies that want to take advantage of India's large market and skilled workforce.

Step-by-Step Process

To participate in the Make in India initiative, companies can follow these steps: 1. Register on the DIPP website and fill out Form MSI-1. 2. Provide details about the project, including the sector, investment amount, and employment generation potential. 3. Upload required documents, such as company registration certificates and project reports. 4. Wait for verification and approval from the DIPP.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

There are no specific deadlines for registering for the Make in India initiative, but companies should apply as soon as possible to take advantage of the incentives. Failure to comply with the program's guidelines and regulations can result in penalties, such as cancellation of incentives and imposition of fines.
