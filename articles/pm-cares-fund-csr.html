PM CARES Fund CSR

The PM CARES Fund is a dedicated national fund created to deal with any kind of emergency or distress situation, like the COVID-19 pandemic, and to provide relief to the affected. The fund is eligible to receive Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) contributions under the Companies Act, 2013. This allows companies to contribute to the PM CARES Fund and claim it as part of their CSR expenditure.

Key Details

The PM CARES Fund was created on March 28, 2020, with the primary objective of dealing with the COVID-19 pandemic. The fund is exempt from income tax under Section 10(46) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, and contributions to the fund are eligible for a 100% tax deduction under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961. Companies can contribute to the PM CARES Fund by filing Form CSR-1 on the MCA21 portal, and the minimum CSR expenditure threshold is 2% of the company's average net profit for the three immediately preceding financial years.

Who Needs This

The PM CARES Fund CSR applies to companies that are required to spend on CSR activities under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013. This includes companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more. NGOs and other organizations can also receive contributions from these companies and utilize them for eligible CSR activities.

Step-by-Step Process

To contribute to the PM CARES Fund, companies need to follow these steps: 1. Calculate their CSR expenditure for the financial year, which is 2% of their average net profit for the three immediately preceding financial years. 2. File Form CSR-1 on the MCA21 portal, specifying the amount to be contributed to the PM CARES Fund. 3. Transfer the contribution to the PM CARES Fund through online payment modes. 4. Obtain a receipt from the PM CARES Fund and maintain records of the contribution.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The deadline for CSR expenditure is the end of the financial year, i.e., March 31. Companies that fail to spend the required amount on CSR activities, including contributions to the PM CARES Fund, may face penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore and imprisonment of up to three years.
