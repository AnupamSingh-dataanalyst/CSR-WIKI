# Institute of Company Secretaries of India
The Institute of Company Secretaries of India (ICSI) is a statutory body established under the Company Secretaries Act, 1980, to regulate and develop the profession of company secretaries in India. The ICSI is responsible for conducting examinations, providing training, and granting certification to company secretaries, as well as promoting and regulating the profession. The institute plays a crucial role in ensuring that companies comply with the provisions of the Companies Act, 2013, and other related laws.

## Key Details
The ICSI has over 50,000 members and 3,000 examination centers across India and abroad. The institute offers various courses, including the Company Secretary (CS) course, which consists of three levels: Foundation, Executive, and Professional. The CS course covers subjects such as company law, corporate governance, and financial management. To become a member of the ICSI, individuals must complete the CS course and undergo a period of practical training.

## Who Needs This
The ICSI is relevant to all companies registered under the Companies Act, 2013, as well as limited liability partnerships (LLPs) and other organizations that require the services of a company secretary. NGOs and non-profit organizations that are registered as companies or LLPs may also need to engage with the ICSI. Additionally, individuals who wish to pursue a career as a company secretary or work in related fields such as corporate law, governance, or compliance may benefit from the ICSI's courses and certification.

## Step-by-Step Process
To become a member of the ICSI, individuals must follow these steps:
1. Register for the CS course on the ICSI website.
2. Complete the Foundation, Executive, and Professional levels of the course.
3. Undergo a period of practical training.
4. Apply for membership with the ICSI.
5. Complete the mandatory continuing professional development (CPD) requirements to maintain membership.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The ICSI has specific deadlines for exam registrations, course completions, and membership renewals. Failure to meet these deadlines may result in penalties, such as late fees or loss of membership. For example, the ICSI requires members to complete a minimum of 30 hours of CPD per year to maintain their membership.
