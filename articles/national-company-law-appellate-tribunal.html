National Company Law Appellate Tribunal

The National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) is a tribunal established under the Companies Act, 2013, to hear appeals against the orders of the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT). It is a quasi-judicial body that operates under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, Government of India. The NCLAT was constituted on June 1, 2016, and is headquartered in New Delhi.

Key Details

The NCLAT has 11 judicial and technical members, including a chairperson, who are appointed by the Government of India. The tribunal has the power to hear appeals against orders passed by the NCLT, including those related to corporate insolvency, mergers and acquisitions, and company law matters. The NCLAT is required to dispose of appeals within a period of 6 months from the date of filing, as per the Companies Act, 2013. Forms such as Form NCLAT-1 and Form NCLAT-2 are used for filing appeals and applications before the NCLAT.

Who Needs This

The NCLAT is relevant to companies, limited liability partnerships, and other corporate entities that are registered under the Companies Act, 2013. It is also relevant to creditors, debtors, and other stakeholders who are affected by the orders of the NCLT. NGOs and charitable organizations that are registered as companies or societies under Indian law may also need to approach the NCLAT in case of disputes or appeals related to company law matters.

Step-by-Step Process

To file an appeal before the NCLAT, the following steps can be followed: - Obtain a certified copy of the order passed by the NCLT - Prepare a petition of appeal in Form NCLAT-1 - Attach relevant documents and annexures to the petition - Pay the required fees and deposit the amount in the prescribed manner - File the petition within the prescribed time limit of 30 or 45 days, depending on the nature of the appeal

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The NCLAT has strict timelines for filing appeals, and any delay can result in the dismissal of the appeal. The tribunal can also impose penalties and costs on parties that fail to comply with its orders or directions. In case of non-compliance with the orders of the NCLAT, companies and individuals can face fines, imprisonment, or other penalties, as prescribed under the Companies Act, 2013.
