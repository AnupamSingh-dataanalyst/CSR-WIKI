# Mitra Jyothi
Mitra Jyothi is a non-governmental organization (NGO) based in Bangalore, India, that works towards empowering people with disabilities, particularly those who are visually impaired. Established in 1990, Mitra Jyothi aims to provide education, employment, and rehabilitation opportunities to the visually impaired, promoting their independence and integration into society. The organization offers a range of services, including computer training, braille transcription, and assistive technology support.

## Key Details
Mitra Jyothi is registered under the Karnataka Societies Registration Act, 1960, and is also registered under Section 12A and 80G of the Income-tax Act, 1961, allowing it to receive tax-exempt donations. The organization is managed by a board of trustees and has a team of experienced staff, including trainers, counselors, and administrators. Mitra Jyothi has partnered with various organizations, including corporations, foundations, and government agencies, to support its programs and services.

## Who Needs This
Mitra Jyothi's services are primarily targeted towards individuals who are visually impaired, including those who are blind or have low vision. The organization's programs and services are also relevant to NGOs and companies that work with people with disabilities, as well as those that are interested in promoting disability inclusion and accessibility. Additionally, Mitra Jyothi's computer training and assistive technology support services may be of interest to companies that are looking to hire people with disabilities or provide accessible technology solutions to their employees.
