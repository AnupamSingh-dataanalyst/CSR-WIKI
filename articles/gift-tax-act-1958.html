Gift Tax Act, 1958

The Gift Tax Act, 1958, is a law that was enacted to tax gifts made by individuals, with the aim of preventing tax evasion and ensuring that gifts are properly accounted for. The Act applies to gifts made on or after April 1, 1958, and it has undergone several amendments over the years to keep pace with changing tax laws and regulations. The Gift Tax Act, 1958, was repealed by the Finance Act, 2004, with effect from October 1, 2004, but its provisions continue to apply to gifts made prior to its repeal.

Key Details

The Gift Tax Act, 1958, defined a gift as any transfer of property, whether movable or immovable, without consideration or for inadequate consideration. The Act applied to gifts exceeding Rs. 3,000 in value, and the tax rate varied depending on the value of the gift. Donors were required to file a gift tax return in Form GT-1, and the due date for filing the return was July 31 of each year.

Who Needs This

The Gift Tax Act, 1958, applied to individuals who made gifts, including non-resident Indians, as well as Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) and companies. Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) that received gifts were also required to report these gifts in their tax returns. However, gifts made to certain exempt organizations, such as charitable trusts and institutions, were not subject to gift tax.

Step-by-Step Process

Although the Gift Tax Act, 1958, has been repealed, individuals who made gifts prior to October 1, 2004, may still need to file a gift tax return. The step-by-step process for filing a gift tax return involves: - Obtaining Form GT-1 from the Income Tax Department or downloading it from the official website - Filling out the form with the required details, including the value of the gift and the recipient's details - Submitting the form to the Income Tax Department by the due date

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The due date for filing a gift tax return was July 31 of each year. Failure to file a return or paying gift tax by the due date could result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1,000 and interest on the tax due.
