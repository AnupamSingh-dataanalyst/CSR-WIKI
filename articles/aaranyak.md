# Aaranyak
Aaranyak is a non-governmental organization (NGO) based in India that works towards conservation of wildlife, particularly in the northeastern region of the country. Established in 2005, Aaranyak focuses on research, conservation, and community engagement to protect the region's rich biodiversity. With its headquarters in Guwahati, Assam, Aaranyak has been instrumental in promoting sustainable livelihoods and conservation practices among local communities.

## Key Details
Aaranyak has been working on various projects, including conservation of the greater adjutant stork, Amur falcon, and Asian elephant, among others. The organization has also been involved in research studies on human-wildlife conflict, wildlife trade, and climate change. Aaranyak has collaborated with various national and international organizations, including the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) and the Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS).

## Who Needs This
Aaranyak's work is relevant to NGOs, companies, and individuals involved in wildlife conservation, research, and community development in India, particularly in the northeastern region. This includes organizations working on projects related to biodiversity conservation, sustainable livelihoods, and environmental education. Companies operating in the region, such as those in the tea, oil, and gas sectors, may also benefit from Aaranyak's expertise and services.
