# CSR Implementing Agency
A CSR Implementing Agency refers to a non-governmental organization (NGO) or foundation that partners with companies to implement Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) projects on their behalf. These agencies play a crucial role in helping companies fulfill their CSR obligations under the Companies Act, 2013. By collaborating with CSR Implementing Agencies, companies can leverage the expertise and resources of these organizations to create meaningful social impact.

## Key Details
To be eligible as a CSR Implementing Agency, an NGO or foundation must be registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, or under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, or as a public trust. The company must also ensure that the implementing agency has a good track record and is capable of undertaking the proposed CSR project. The CSR expenses incurred by the company must be disclosed in the Directors' Report, using Form AOC-2, and the CSR Policy must be displayed on the company's website.

## Who Needs This
This applies to companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, who are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities. NGOs and foundations that wish to partner with these companies as CSR Implementing Agencies must also be aware of the requirements and guidelines.

## Step-by-Step Process
The process of becoming a CSR Implementing Agency involves several steps, including registration under the relevant Act, obtaining a unique ID from the National CSR Portal, and building a track record of successful projects. Companies must also conduct due diligence on potential implementing agencies, evaluate their proposals, and enter into a formal agreement outlining the scope, timelines, and budget for the CSR project.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies are required to spend the prescribed amount on CSR activities within the financial year, and any unspent amount must be transferred to a CSR Fund or a registered trust within six months of the financial year. Failure to comply with the CSR provisions can result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore and imprisonment of up to three years for the company's directors.
