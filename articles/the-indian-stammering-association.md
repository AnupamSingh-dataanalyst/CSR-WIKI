# The Indian Stammering Association
The Indian Stammering Association (ISA) is a non-profit organization that aims to support and empower individuals who stutter, also known as people who stammer. The ISA works to create awareness about stuttering, provides a platform for people who stammer to share their experiences, and advocates for their rights. Founded in 1999, the ISA has been instrumental in promoting inclusivity and acceptance of people who stammer in Indian society.

## Key Details
The Indian Stammering Association has over 500 members across India and has chapters in several cities, including Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore. The ISA organizes regular meetings, workshops, and conferences to bring together people who stammer and provide them with a platform to share their experiences and learn from each other. The ISA also collaborates with other organizations, such as the Self Help Groups of People Who Stutter (SHG) and the National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences (NIMHANS), to promote research and awareness about stuttering.

## Who Needs This
The Indian Stammering Association is relevant to individuals who stammer, as well as to non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and companies that work with people with disabilities. NGOs that focus on disability rights, education, and healthcare may find it useful to collaborate with the ISA to promote inclusivity and accessibility for people who stammer. Companies that aim to create a diverse and inclusive workplace may also benefit from partnering with the ISA to provide support and resources to employees who stammer.
