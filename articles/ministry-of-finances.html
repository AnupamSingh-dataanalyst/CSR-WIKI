Ministry of Finance's Role in Income Tax

The Ministry of Finance plays a crucial role in governing income tax in India, with the power to tax non-agricultural income derived from Entry 82 of the Union List of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India. The income-tax law is comprised of the Income-tax Act, 1961, Income Tax Rules 1962, Notifications and Circulars issued by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT), annual Finance Acts, and judicial pronouncements by the Supreme and high courts of India. This framework provides the foundation for income tax regulation in India.

Key Details

The Income-tax Act, 1961, is the primary legislation governing income tax in India, with Section 10(1) defining agricultural income. The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) issues Notifications and Circulars to clarify and implement the income-tax law. Key forms used in income tax filing include the ITR-1 (Sahaj) for individuals, ITR-4 (Sugam) for presumptive income, and ITR-6 for companies. The income tax slabs and rates are revised annually through the Finance Acts.

Who Needs This

This applies to all individuals, companies, and organizations that earn income in India, including non-resident Indians and foreign companies with a presence in India. NGOs and companies must comply with income tax regulations, including filing returns and paying taxes on time. Specifically, this includes private limited companies, public limited companies, partnerships, sole proprietorships, and trusts.

Step-by-Step Process

The step-by-step process for income tax filing involves registering on the Income Tax Department's portal, obtaining a PAN (Permanent Account Number), and filing the relevant ITR form. The process includes: - Registering on the Income Tax Department's portal (www.incometax.gov.in) - Obtaining a PAN (Permanent Account Number) - Filing the relevant ITR form (e.g., ITR-1, ITR-4, or ITR-6) - Paying any due taxes or claiming refunds - Verifying the return through Aadhaar OTP, EVC, or physical signature

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The deadline for filing income tax returns is typically July 31st of each year, with penalties for late filing, including a fine of up to Rs. 10,000. Additionally, interest may be charged on unpaid taxes. It is essential to file returns and pay taxes on time to avoid these penalties.
