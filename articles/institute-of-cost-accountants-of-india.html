Institute of Cost Accountants of India

The Institute of Cost Accountants of India (ICAI) is a statutory body established under the Cost and Works Accountants Act, 1959, to regulate the profession of cost accounting in India. The ICAI is responsible for promoting, regulating, and developing the profession of cost accountants, and it plays a crucial role in the administration of the Companies Act, 2013, and other related laws. The institute is headquartered in Kolkata and has chapters and branches across India.

Key Details

The ICAI has over 80,000 members and more than 300,000 students, making it one of the largest professional accounting bodies in India. The institute offers various courses, including the Cost and Management Accounting (CMA) course, which is a three-level course that includes Foundation, Intermediate, and Final exams. The ICAI is also responsible for maintaining the register of cost accountants, which includes the names of all members who are eligible to practice as cost accountants in India. To become a member of the ICAI, an individual must fill out Form 2, which is available on the ICAI website, and submit it along with the required fees and documents.

Who Needs This

The ICAI is relevant to all companies and NGOs that require the services of a cost accountant, including those that are required to maintain cost accounting records under the Companies Act, 2013. This includes companies with a turnover of more than Rs. 35 crores, as well as companies that are engaged in certain industries, such as telecommunications and insurance. Additionally, NGOs that receive funding from the government or other sources may also require the services of a cost accountant to ensure that their financial statements are prepared in accordance with the applicable laws and regulations.

Step-by-Step Process

To become a member of the ICAI, an individual must follow these steps: 1. Meet the eligibility criteria, which includes having a degree from a recognized university and completing the CMA course. 2. Fill out Form 2, which is available on the ICAI website, and submit it along with the required fees and documents. 3. Appear for the oral examination, if required. 4. Pay the membership fees, which currently stand at Rs. 20,000 for new members. 5. Complete the mandatory continuing professional development (CPD) hours, which are required to maintain membership.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The ICAI has strict deadlines for submitting various forms and documents, including Form 2, which must be submitted within 30 days of completing the CMA course. Failure to comply with these deadlines can result in penalties, including fines and suspension of membership. Additionally, members who fail to complete the mandatory CPD hours can face penalties, including suspension of membership.
