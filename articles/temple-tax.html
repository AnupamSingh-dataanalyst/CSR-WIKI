Temple tax

Temple tax refers to the tax exemption and related provisions applicable to temples and other religious institutions in India, which are governed by specific sections of the Income-tax Act, 1961. The concept of temple tax is closely related to the income tax laws in India, which distinguish between taxable and exempt income. Religious institutions, including temples, are subject to specific tax rules and regulations.

Key Details

The Income-tax Act, 1961, provides exemptions to religious institutions, including temples, under Section 10(23B) and Section 11. To claim exemption, temples must file their income tax returns using Form ITR-7 and maintain proper accounts and records. The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) issues notifications and circulars to clarify the tax provisions applicable to temples.

Who Needs This

Temple tax provisions apply to all religious institutions, including temples, mosques, churches, and gurudwaras, that have taxable income. NGOs and companies that donate to temples or other religious institutions may also need to understand these provisions to claim tax deductions. Additionally, trusts and societies that manage temple affairs must comply with the income tax laws and regulations.

Step-by-Step Process

To claim tax exemption, temples must follow these steps: 1. Register with the income tax department and obtain a PAN (Permanent Account Number). 2. File Form ITR-7 and attach the required documents, including audited accounts and balance sheets. 3. Maintain proper records and accounts to support the exemption claim. 4. Ensure compliance with the provisions of Section 11 and Section 10(23B) of the Income-tax Act, 1961.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The due date for filing the income tax return for temples is September 30th of each year. Failure to file the return or non-compliance with the tax provisions may result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 lakh, and loss of exemption.
