Ibtada

Ibtada is a non-governmental organization (NGO) based in Alwar, Rajasthan, India, that works towards empowering women and girls through education and economic development. Established in 2000, Ibtada aims to address the social and economic inequalities faced by women in rural India. The organization's name, "Ibtada," which means "beginning" in Urdu, reflects its focus on creating a new beginning for women and girls in the region.

Key Details

Ibtada's programs focus on education, livelihoods, and health, with a specific emphasis on adolescent girls. The organization works with over 10,000 women and girls in 100 villages across Alwar district, providing them with access to education, vocational training, and economic opportunities. Ibtada's initiatives include the formation of self-help groups, vocational training centers, and education programs for out-of-school girls.

Who Needs This

Ibtada's programs and services are designed for NGOs and companies working in the areas of women's empowerment, education, and economic development in rural India. Specifically, this applies to organizations working with adolescent girls, women's self-help groups, and community-based initiatives. Companies with corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives focused on women's empowerment and education may also find Ibtada's work relevant.
