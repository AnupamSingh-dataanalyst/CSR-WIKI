Child In Need Institute

The Child In Need Institute (CINI) is a non-governmental organization based in India that works to improve the lives of underprivileged children, particularly those affected by poverty, hunger, and lack of access to education and healthcare. CINI was founded in 1974 by Dr. Samir Chaudhuri and has since become a leading organization in the field of child development and welfare in India. With a focus on sustainable development and community empowerment, CINI aims to create a brighter future for India's most vulnerable children.

Key Details

CINI operates in several states across India, including West Bengal, Jharkhand, and Uttar Pradesh, and has a strong presence in over 1,000 villages. The organization works with local communities, governments, and other stakeholders to provide a range of services, including healthcare, education, and livelihood support. CINI's programs are designed to address the specific needs of children and families in each region, with a focus on long-term sustainability and impact.

Who Needs This

CINI's services and support are available to non-governmental organizations (NGOs), community-based organizations, and corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives that work with children and families in India. NGOs and companies that are registered under the Indian Companies Act, 2013, and are compliant with the CSR provisions under Section 135 of the Act, may be eligible to partner with CINI or seek funding for their projects. Additionally, organizations that are registered under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, and have a valid FCRA registration, may also be eligible to receive foreign funding for their projects.

Step-by-Step Process

To partner with CINI or seek funding for a project, NGOs and companies can follow these steps: 1. Review CINI's eligibility criteria and ensure that their organization meets the requirements. 2. Submit a proposal outlining their project goals, objectives, and budget. 3. Wait for CINI's review and approval process, which may take several weeks or months. 4. Once approved, work with CINI's team to develop a detailed project plan and budget. 5. Implement the project and provide regular progress reports to CINI.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

CINI has specific deadlines for proposal submissions, which are typically announced on their website and social media channels. NGOs and companies that miss these deadlines may need to wait for the next funding cycle, which could be several months away. Additionally, organizations that fail to comply with CINI's reporting requirements or misuse funds may face penalties, including termination of their partnership or funding agreement.
