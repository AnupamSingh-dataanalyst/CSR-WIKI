# DARPAN Registration
DARPAN (Darpan) registration is a unique identification number provided to non-profit organizations, including NGOs, by the NITI Aayog portal. This registration is mandatory for NGOs to receive grants from government ministries and departments. The DARPAN portal aims to bring transparency and accountability in the funding of NGOs.

## Key Details
The DARPAN registration process involves filling out an online application form on the NITI Aayog portal, which requires basic information about the NGO, such as its name, address, and objectives. The registration is free of cost, and the unique ID generated is a 10-digit number. NGOs are required to submit their audited annual accounts and annual reports on the DARPAN portal.

## Who Needs This
DARPAN registration is mandatory for all NGOs that receive funding from the government, either directly or indirectly. This includes NGOs that work in various sectors such as education, healthcare, and social welfare. Companies that have a corporate social responsibility (CSR) arm and partner with NGOs to implement their CSR initiatives also need to ensure that their partner NGOs have a DARPAN registration.

## Step-by-Step Process
To obtain a DARPAN registration, NGOs can follow these steps:
1. Visit the NITI Aayog portal and click on the DARPAN registration link.
2. Fill out the online application form, providing basic information about the NGO.
3. Upload the required documents, including the registration certificate, PAN card, and audited annual accounts.
4. Submit the application form and wait for the unique ID to be generated.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
NGOs are required to submit their audited annual accounts and annual reports on the DARPAN portal within 6 months of the end of the financial year. Failure to do so may result in the NGO's registration being suspended or cancelled.
