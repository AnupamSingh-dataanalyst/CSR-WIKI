Church's Auxiliary for Social Action

The Church's Auxiliary for Social Action (CASA) is a non-governmental organization (NGO) in India that works towards the empowerment of marginalized communities, with a focus on disaster response, sustainable livelihoods, and social justice. Established in 1947, CASA has been actively involved in various social and humanitarian initiatives across the country. With its headquarters in Delhi, CASA has been a key player in promoting community development and providing support to vulnerable populations.

Key Details

CASA is registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, and is also registered with the Ministry of Home Affairs under the Foreign Contribution Regulation Act (FCRA), 2010. As per the FCRA, 2010, CASA is required to file an annual return in Form FC-4, which includes details of foreign contributions received and utilized during the year. CASA also needs to maintain audited accounts and submit them to the authorities as per the provisions of the Income-tax Act, 1961.

Who Needs This

CASA's services and support are primarily targeted towards marginalized communities, including rural poor, women, children, and disaster-affected populations. NGOs and companies working in the areas of disaster response, sustainable livelihoods, and social justice may find it beneficial to collaborate with CASA or seek their expertise. Additionally, organizations seeking to comply with the provisions of the Companies Act, 2013, and the Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) rules may also find CASA's work relevant.

Step-by-Step Process

To collaborate with CASA or seek their support, NGOs and companies can follow these steps: 1. Visit the CASA website to understand their work and areas of focus. 2. Reach out to CASA's headquarters or regional offices to discuss potential collaboration opportunities. 3. Submit a proposal outlining the objectives, scope, and expected outcomes of the proposed collaboration. 4. CASA will review the proposal and respond with their decision, which may include a request for additional information or a meeting to discuss further.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

As a registered NGO, CASA is required to comply with various regulatory requirements, including filing annual returns and maintaining audited accounts. Failure to comply with these requirements may result in penalties, including fines and cancellation of registration. For example, under the FCRA, 2010, failure to file an annual return in Form FC-4 may attract a penalty of Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 10,000.
