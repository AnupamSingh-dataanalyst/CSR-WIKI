# List of Acts of the Parliament of India
The List of Acts of the Parliament of India is a comprehensive compilation of laws enacted by the Parliament of India, which is the supreme legislative body in the country. These acts govern various aspects of Indian society, economy, and governance, and are essential for NGOs, companies, and individuals to understand and comply with. The list includes acts such as the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, which regulates foreign funding for certain entities.

## Key Details
The List of Acts of the Parliament of India includes over 1,500 acts, with new ones being added regularly. Some key acts that are relevant to NGOs and companies include the Companies Act, 2013, the Income-tax Act, 1961, and the Goods and Services Tax (GST) Act, 2017. These acts often involve specific forms, such as Form FC-1 for foreign contribution registration, and thresholds, like the Rs. 1 crore threshold for GST registration.

## Who Needs This
NGOs, companies, and individuals who operate in India need to be aware of the List of Acts of the Parliament of India, as these laws govern various aspects of their operations. Specifically, entities that receive foreign funding, such as NGOs and research institutions, need to comply with the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010. Companies, on the other hand, need to comply with the Companies Act, 2013, and the GST Act, 2017.

## Step-by-Step Process
While there is no specific step-by-step process for complying with the List of Acts of the Parliament of India, entities can follow these general steps:
* Identify the relevant acts that apply to their operations
* Register with the relevant authorities, such as the Ministry of Corporate Affairs or the GST Network
* File necessary forms and returns, such as Form FC-1 or GST returns
* Comply with the provisions of the relevant acts, such as maintaining accounts and records

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Entities that fail to comply with the List of Acts of the Parliament of India can face penalties, such as fines and imprisonment. For example, non-compliance with the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, can result in a penalty of up to Rs. 1 lakh and/or imprisonment for up to 3 years. Important deadlines include the annual filing of GST returns and the quarterly filing of foreign contribution returns.
