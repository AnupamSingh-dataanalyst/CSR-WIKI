CSR Concepts

CSR Committee

A CSR Committee is a board-level committee required under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, to oversee and implement Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives of a company. The committee plays a crucial role in ensuring that the company's CSR activities are aligned with its business strategy and contribute to the well-being of society. The CSR Committee is responsible for recommending the CSR policy, outlining the CSR activities, and monitoring the implementation of these activities.

Key Details

The CSR Committee should consist of at least three directors, with at least one independent director. The committee should formulate and recommend to the Board a CSR policy which outlines the activities to be undertaken by the company as part of its CSR initiatives. The CSR policy should include the company's CSR vision, mission, and objectives, as well as the CSR activities to be undertaken, the amount to be spent on these activities, and the implementation schedule. Companies are required to file Form CSR-1 with the Registrar of Companies to provide details of their CSR activities.

Who Needs This

The CSR Committee is mandatory for companies that meet certain criteria, including companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crores or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1000 crores or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crores or more. This applies to both public and private companies, as well as foreign companies with a branch or project office in India. NGOs that receive CSR funding from companies are also indirectly affected by the CSR Committee, as they need to ensure that the company's CSR activities are aligned with their own goals and objectives.

Step-by-Step Process

To establish a CSR Committee, companies should follow these steps: 1. Identify the directors who will be part of the CSR Committee, ensuring that at least one is an independent director. 2. Formulate a CSR policy that outlines the company's CSR vision, mission, and objectives, as well as the CSR activities to be undertaken. 3. Recommend the CSR policy to the Board for approval. 4. File Form CSR-1 with the Registrar of Companies to provide details of the company's CSR activities.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits for the preceding three years on CSR activities. If a company fails to spend the required amount, it must provide reasons for not doing so in its Directors' Report. Failure to comply with CSR regulations can result in penalties, including fines and imprisonment.

Related Topics

  • CSR Policy: The company's CSR vision, mission, and objectives.
  • Section 135: The section of the Companies Act, 2013, that deals with CSR.
  • Schedule VII: The schedule of the Companies Act, 2013, that lists the activities that can be undertaken as part of CSR initiatives.
  • MCA21 Portal: The online portal where companies can file Form CSR-1 and other CSR-related forms.
  • NGO Registration: The process by which NGOs can register to receive CSR funding from companies.
