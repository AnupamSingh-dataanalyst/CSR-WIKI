# Indian Institute of Corporate Affairs
The Indian Institute of Corporate Affairs (IICA) is a premier institution established by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) to cater to the needs of corporate governance, policy, and law. It aims to provide a platform for discussions, debates, and capacity-building programs to promote good corporate governance practices among stakeholders. The IICA plays a crucial role in implementing the provisions of the Companies Act, 2013, and other related laws.

## Key Details
The Indian Institute of Corporate Affairs was established on March 29, 2008, as a society under the Societies Registration Act, 1860. It is headquartered in Manesar, Gurgaon, and has a campus spread over 14 acres. The IICA offers various programs, including the Corporate Governance Certification Program, the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) Certification Program, and the National Foundation for Corporate Governance (NFCG) Fellowship Program. The institute also conducts research and publishes reports on corporate governance, insolvency, and other related topics.

## Who Needs This
The Indian Institute of Corporate Affairs is relevant to various stakeholders, including companies, NGOs, and individuals involved in corporate governance, insolvency, and related fields. This includes:
* Companies registered under the Companies Act, 2013
* Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs) registered under the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008
* Insolvency professionals and stakeholders involved in the insolvency resolution process
* NGOs and non-profit organizations involved in corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives
