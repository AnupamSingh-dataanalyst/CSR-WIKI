# List of non-governmental organisations in India
The list of non-governmental organisations (NGOs) in India is a vast and diverse collection of entities working towards various social, economic, and environmental causes. These NGOs play a crucial role in addressing the country's developmental challenges and are regulated by various laws, including the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010. The FCRA, 2010, aims to regulate the acceptance and utilisation of foreign contribution or foreign hospitality by certain individuals or associations or companies.

## Key Details
The FCRA, 2010, requires NGOs to register with the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) to receive foreign contributions. The registration is valid for a period of 5 years, and NGOs need to renew it before the expiry date. The MHA has introduced the FCRA online portal for NGOs to submit their applications, renewals, and annual returns. NGOs need to file Form FC-3 for registration, Form FC-4 for prior permission, and Form FC-6 for annual returns.

## Who Needs This
NGOs, associations, and companies that intend to receive foreign contributions or foreign hospitality need to comply with the FCRA, 2010. This includes entities working in the fields of education, healthcare, social welfare, human rights, and environmental conservation. Companies that have a significant presence in India and receive foreign funding also need to adhere to the regulations under the FCRA, 2010.

## Step-by-Step Process
To register under the FCRA, 2010, NGOs need to follow these steps:
1. Create an account on the FCRA online portal.
2. Fill and submit Form FC-3, along with the required documents, including the NGO's registration certificate, Memorandum of Association, and audited financial statements.
3. Pay the registration fee of ₹2,000 for 5 years.
4. Wait for the MHA to verify and approve the application.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
NGOs need to renew their FCRA registration before the expiry date to avoid penalties. The MHA may impose a penalty of up to ₹1 lakh for non-renewal or non-compliance with the regulations. NGOs also need to file their annual returns in Form FC-6 within 9 months of the end of the financial year.
