# CSR Expenditure Calculation
Calculating the Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) expenditure is a crucial aspect for companies in India, as it determines the amount they need to spend on CSR activities. The CSR expenditure is calculated based on the company's net profit, and it is mandatory for companies to spend at least 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities. This calculation is governed by the Companies Act, 2013, and the CSR Policy Rules, 2014.

## Key Details
The CSR expenditure is calculated as 2% of the average net profit of the company made during the three immediately preceding financial years. The net profit is calculated as per Section 198 of the Companies Act, 2013. Companies can calculate their CSR expenditure using the formula: CSR Expenditure = 2% of (Net Profit of FY1 + Net Profit of FY2 + Net Profit of FY3) / 3. The CSR expenditure should be spent on activities specified in Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013, and companies need to file Form CSR-2 with the Registrar of Companies (RoC) to disclose their CSR expenditure.

## Who Needs This
This applies to companies that have a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more during any financial year. These companies need to constitute a CSR Committee and formulate a CSR Policy, which outlines their CSR activities and expenditure. NGOs that partner with these companies to implement CSR projects also need to be aware of the CSR expenditure calculation to ensure that the projects are aligned with the company's CSR obligations.

## Step-by-Step Process
To calculate the CSR expenditure, companies need to follow these steps:
1. Calculate the net profit for each of the three preceding financial years.
2. Calculate the average net profit by adding the net profits of the three years and dividing by 3.
3. Calculate 2% of the average net profit to determine the CSR expenditure.
4. Identify the CSR activities to be undertaken and allocate the CSR expenditure accordingly.
5. File Form CSR-2 with the RoC to disclose the CSR expenditure.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies need to spend the CSR expenditure within the financial year, and any unspent amount needs to be transferred to a CSR fund or a registered trust. If a company fails to spend the CSR expenditure, it needs to provide reasons for the same in its Board's report. Failure to comply with the CSR provisions can result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore.
