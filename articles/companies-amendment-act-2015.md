# Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015
The Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015 is a legislation in India that aims to improve the ease of doing business in the country by making significant changes to the Companies Act, 2013. This amendment act was enacted to address various issues and concerns raised by stakeholders after the implementation of the Companies Act, 2013. The amendments focus on simplifying procedures, reducing regulatory burdens, and promoting corporate governance.

## Key Details
The Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015 received the President's assent on May 26, 2015, and was notified in the Official Gazette on May 29, 2015. The amendment act introduced changes to 22 sections of the Companies Act, 2013, and also introduced a new section, Section 458A, which deals with the exemption from the provisions of the Act for certain classes of companies. Some of the key amendments include the removal of the requirement for a minimum paid-up share capital for private companies, the introduction of a new concept of "deposit" to replace the term "deposit" and "loan" in the Act, and the simplification of the procedure for the appointment of directors.

## Who Needs This
The Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015 applies to all companies registered under the Companies Act, 2013, including private companies, public companies, and one-person companies. Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) that are registered as companies under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, are also required to comply with the provisions of the amendment act. Additionally, companies that are required to file forms and returns with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) under the Companies Act, 2013, such as Form MGT-14 and Form AOC-4, need to be aware of the changes introduced by the amendment act.

## Step-by-Step Process
While there is no specific step-by-step process for implementing the Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015, companies and NGOs need to ensure that they are in compliance with the amended provisions of the Companies Act, 2013. This includes reviewing and updating their Memorandum and Articles of Association, ensuring that they are in compliance with the new requirements for the appointment of directors, and filing the necessary forms and returns with the MCA.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015 does not specify any particular deadlines for compliance. However, companies and NGOs that fail to comply with the amended provisions of the Companies Act, 2013, may be subject to penalties and fines under the Act. The MCA may also take action against companies that fail to file the necessary forms and returns, including striking off the name of the company from the register of companies.
