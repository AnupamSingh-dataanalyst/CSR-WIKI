# CSR Impact Assessment
CSR Impact Assessment is a mandatory evaluation process for large Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) projects in India, aimed at measuring the social, economic, and environmental impact of these projects. This assessment is conducted by an independent third-party agency to ensure transparency and accountability in CSR spending. The assessment helps companies and NGOs understand the effectiveness of their CSR initiatives and identify areas for improvement.

## Key Details
The CSR Impact Assessment is applicable to CSR projects with an outlay of Rs. 10 crore or more, and the assessment should be conducted through an independent external agency. The assessment report should be in the format specified by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, and it should be annexed to the CSR policy of the company. The report should include details such as the project's objectives, outcomes, and impact, as well as the methodology used for the assessment.

## Who Needs This
The CSR Impact Assessment is mandatory for companies that have a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, and that spend Rs. 10 crore or more on CSR projects in a financial year. This applies to both Indian companies and foreign companies having a branch or project office in India. NGOs that receive CSR funding from such companies may also be involved in the assessment process.

## Step-by-Step Process
The step-by-step process for CSR Impact Assessment involves the following steps: 
1. Identification of the CSR project for impact assessment
2. Selection of an independent external agency for conducting the assessment
3. Conducting the impact assessment using a predefined methodology
4. Preparation of the impact assessment report
5. Annexing the report to the company's CSR policy

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The CSR Impact Assessment report should be prepared and annexed to the company's CSR policy within 6 months from the end of the financial year in which the CSR project was completed. Failure to comply with the CSR Impact Assessment requirements may result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore, as per Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013.
