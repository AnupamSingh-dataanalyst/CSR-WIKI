# Tax Shelter
A tax shelter is a financial arrangement or investment that reduces or defers an individual's or organization's tax liability, allowing them to minimize their tax payments. In India, tax shelters are governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and are subject to various rules and regulations. The primary goal of a tax shelter is to provide a legitimate means of reducing tax liability, while also ensuring compliance with Indian tax laws.

## Key Details
Tax shelters in India can take various forms, including investments in tax-saving instruments, such as Public Provident Fund (PPF), National Savings Certificate (NSC), and Equity-Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS) mutual funds. The Income-tax Act, 1961, specifies various deductions and exemptions, such as Section 80C, Section 80D, and Section 80G, which can be claimed to reduce tax liability. For example, investments up to Rs. 1.5 lakh in eligible instruments can be claimed as a deduction under Section 80C.

## Who Needs This
Tax shelters are relevant to various individuals and organizations in India, including salaried employees, business owners, and non-governmental organizations (NGOs). NGOs, in particular, can benefit from tax shelters, such as Section 80G, which provides a deduction for donations made to eligible charitable institutions. Companies can also claim deductions for charitable contributions and investments in tax-saving instruments.

## Step-by-Step Process
To claim a tax shelter, individuals and organizations must follow a step-by-step process:
1. Identify eligible tax-saving instruments or charitable institutions.
2. Invest or donate within the specified limits and deadlines.
3. Maintain documentation, such as receipts and certificates, to support the claim.
4. File the income tax return (ITR) using the appropriate form, such as ITR-1 or ITR-4, and claim the deduction or exemption.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadline for filing income tax returns in India is typically July 31st of each year, although this may be extended in certain circumstances. Failure to file returns or claim deductions on time can result in penalties, such as a fine of Rs. 5,000, and interest on the outstanding tax liability.
