# CSR Policy India
The CSR Policy in India is a framework under the Companies Act 2013 that mandates certain companies to spend a portion of their profits on corporate social responsibility activities. This policy aims to promote social and economic development and encourage companies to contribute to the well-being of society. The CSR Policy is governed by Section 135 of the Companies Act 2013 and the Companies (Corporate Social Responsibility Policy) Rules 2014.

## Key Details
The CSR Policy applies to companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crores or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1000 crores or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crores or more. These companies are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities. The CSR activities should be undertaken as per the Schedule VII of the Companies Act 2013, which includes activities such as eradicating hunger, poverty, and malnutrition, promoting education, and promoting environmental sustainability. Companies are required to file Form CSR-1 with the Registrar of Companies (RoC) to disclose their CSR policy and Form CSR-2 to disclose their CSR expenditure.

## Who Needs This
The CSR Policy applies to all companies, including private and public companies, that meet the specified thresholds. This includes companies registered under the Companies Act 2013, as well as foreign companies having a branch or project office in India. NGOs and other organizations that partner with companies to implement CSR activities should also be aware of the CSR Policy and its requirements. Additionally, companies that do not meet the specified thresholds but wish to undertake CSR activities voluntarily can also adopt a CSR policy.

## Step-by-Step Process
To implement a CSR policy, companies should follow these steps:
1. Conduct a CSR needs assessment to identify the areas where the company can make a positive impact.
2. Form a CSR committee consisting of at least three directors, with at least one independent director.
3. Develop a CSR policy that outlines the company's CSR objectives, activities, and budget.
4. Approve the CSR policy and budget at a board meeting.
5. Implement the CSR activities and monitor their progress.
6. File the necessary forms with the RoC, including Form CSR-1 and Form CSR-2.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies are required to file Form CSR-1 within 30 days of adopting their CSR policy, and Form CSR-2 within 30 days of the end of the financial year. Failure to comply with the CSR Policy requirements can result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore and imprisonment of up to three years.
