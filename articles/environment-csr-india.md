# Environment CSR India
Environment CSR in India refers to the corporate social responsibility initiatives undertaken by companies to mitigate their environmental footprint and contribute to sustainable development. These initiatives include afforestation, clean energy, waste management, and other eco-friendly activities. The environment is one of the key focus areas under the Companies Act, 2013, which mandates companies to spend a certain percentage of their profits on CSR activities.

## Key Details
The Companies Act, 2013, mandates companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, to spend at least 2% of their average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities. Environment CSR activities can be undertaken through registered trusts, societies, or Section 8 companies, and companies can also collaborate with NGOs, research institutions, and government agencies to implement their environment CSR projects. The Ministry of Corporate Affairs has notified the CSR Rules, 2014, which provide guidelines for companies to undertake CSR activities, including environment CSR.

## Who Needs This
Environment CSR applies to companies operating in India that meet the threshold criteria specified under the Companies Act, 2013. This includes public and private companies, limited liability partnerships, and foreign companies having a branch or project office in India. NGOs and research institutions working in the environment sector can also partner with companies to implement environment CSR projects. Companies can undertake environment CSR activities in various areas, including afforestation, clean energy, waste management, conservation of natural resources, and climate change mitigation.

## Step-by-Step Process
To undertake environment CSR activities, companies can follow these steps:
1. Identify the environment CSR project or activity to be undertaken.
2. Form a CSR committee to oversee the implementation of the project.
3. Prepare a detailed project proposal, including the budget, timeline, and expected outcomes.
4. Collaborate with NGOs, research institutions, or government agencies, if necessary.
5. Obtain necessary approvals and permissions from the relevant authorities.
6. Implement the project and monitor its progress.
7. Submit the CSR report in Form CSR-2 to the Ministry of Corporate Affairs through the MCA21 portal.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies are required to spend the mandated 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities, including environment CSR, during each financial year. The CSR report in Form CSR-2 must be submitted to the Ministry of Corporate Affairs within 30 days of the end of the financial year. Failure to comply with the CSR provisions can result in penalties, including fines and imprisonment.
