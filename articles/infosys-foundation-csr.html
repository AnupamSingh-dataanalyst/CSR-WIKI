Infosys Foundation CSR

The Infosys Foundation is the philanthropic arm of Infosys, a leading Indian IT company, and is responsible for implementing the company's Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) programs. Through the foundation, Infosys aims to support healthcare, education, arts and culture, and rural development initiatives across India. The foundation's CSR initiatives are guided by the Indian Companies Act, 2013, and the Companies (Corporate Social Responsibility Policy) Rules, 2014.

Key Details

The Infosys Foundation focuses on several key areas, including: * Healthcare: Providing medical aid and support to underprivileged communities * Education: Supporting educational institutions and initiatives, particularly in rural areas * Arts and Culture: Promoting Indian arts, culture, and heritage * Rural Development: Implementing initiatives for rural development, including water conservation, sanitation, and livelihood generation The foundation's CSR budget is determined based on the company's net profit, as per the Companies Act, 2013, which mandates that companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, allocate at least 2% of their average net profit for CSR activities.

Who Needs This

This information is relevant to NGOs and companies that are interested in collaborating with the Infosys Foundation for CSR initiatives. Specifically, it applies to: * NGOs working in the areas of healthcare, education, arts and culture, and rural development * Companies looking to partner with the Infosys Foundation for CSR initiatives * Social enterprises and community organizations seeking funding or support for their projects

Step-by-Step Process

To collaborate with the Infosys Foundation, NGOs and companies can follow these steps: 1. Review the foundation's focus areas and ensure alignment with their own goals and objectives 2. Submit a proposal outlining their project plan, budget, and expected outcomes 3. Wait for the foundation's review and approval process, which may involve due diligence and site visits 4. Sign a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the foundation, outlining the terms and conditions of the partnership

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The Infosys Foundation typically accepts proposals throughout the year, but the review and approval process may take several months. NGOs and companies should plan accordingly and submit their proposals well in advance of their project start date. Failure to comply with the foundation's guidelines and reporting requirements may result in termination of the partnership.
