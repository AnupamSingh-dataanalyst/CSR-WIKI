80G Tax Exemption

The 80G tax exemption is a provision under the Income-tax Act, 1961, that allows donors to claim a deduction for donations made to registered non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and charitable institutions. This exemption aims to encourage philanthropy and support the social sector in India. By obtaining 80G certification, NGOs can attract more donors and increase their fundraising potential.

Key Details

To be eligible for 80G tax exemption, an NGO must be registered under the Income-tax Act, 1961, and must have obtained a certificate from the Income Tax Department. The certificate is typically valid for a period of 5 years, after which the NGO must reapply. The application for 80G certification is made in Form 10G, and the NGO must submit its audited accounts, annual report, and other relevant documents to support its application. The exemption limit under Section 80G is 50% of the donor's taxable income, subject to a maximum limit of 10% of the donor's gross total income.

Who Needs This

The 80G tax exemption applies to all NGOs and charitable institutions that are registered under the Income-tax Act, 1961, and are engaged in social welfare activities, such as education, healthcare, poverty alleviation, and environmental conservation. This includes trusts, societies, and section 8 companies that are working towards the betterment of society. Donors who make contributions to these NGOs can also claim the 80G tax exemption, subject to the limits and conditions specified under the Income-tax Act.

Step-by-Step Process

To obtain 80G certification, an NGO must follow these steps: 1. Register under the Income-tax Act, 1961, by obtaining a Permanent Account Number (PAN) and filing the necessary income tax returns. 2. Apply for 80G certification in Form 10G, along with the required documents, such as audited accounts, annual report, and trust deed or memorandum of association. 3. Submit the application to the Income Tax Department, along with the prescribed fee. 4. Wait for the Income Tax Department to verify the application and issue the 80G certificate.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The application for 80G certification must be made within 6 months from the date of registration of the NGO. If the NGO fails to apply within this timeframe, it may lose its eligibility for 80G certification. Additionally, if the NGO fails to comply with the conditions specified under the Income-tax Act, its 80G certification may be revoked, and it may be subject to penalties and fines.
