# Ashraya
Ashraya is a scheme introduced by the Government of India to provide support and protection to destitute women, orphans, and other vulnerable groups. The scheme aims to provide shelter, food, clothing, and other basic necessities to those in need. It is implemented through various non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and government agencies.

## Key Details
The Ashraya scheme provides a range of benefits, including financial assistance, medical care, and educational support, to eligible beneficiaries. The scheme is funded by the Government of India and implemented through the Ministry of Women and Child Development. To be eligible for the scheme, beneficiaries must meet certain criteria, such as being a destitute woman or orphan, and having an annual income below a certain threshold.

## Who Needs This
The Ashraya scheme is applicable to NGOs and organizations working with destitute women, orphans, and other vulnerable groups. Companies and corporate entities can also contribute to the scheme through their corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives, as per Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, and Schedule VII of the Act. NGOs and companies can partner with the Government to implement the scheme and provide support to beneficiaries.

## Step-by-Step Process
To implement the Ashraya scheme, NGOs and companies can follow these steps:
1. Register with the National Corporate Social Responsibility Authority (NCSRA) portal, if applicable.
2. Identify eligible beneficiaries and conduct a needs assessment to determine the type of support required.
3. Develop a project proposal outlining the objectives, scope, and budget for the project.
4. Submit the proposal to the relevant government agency or funding authority for approval.
5. Implement the project and provide support to beneficiaries, as per the approved proposal.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadlines and penalties for the Ashraya scheme vary depending on the specific implementation agency and funding authority. Generally, NGOs and companies are required to submit annual reports and audited accounts to the funding authority, as per the terms of the agreement. Failure to comply with the reporting requirements can result in penalties, such as withdrawal of funding or cancellation of the project.
