# Greenpeace India
Greenpeace India is a non-governmental organization that aims to promote environmental protection and conservation in India. As a recipient of foreign funding, Greenpeace India has been subject to the regulations under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, which oversees the acceptance and utilization of foreign contributions by certain individuals, associations, or companies. The organization has been at the center of controversy and regulatory scrutiny due to its receipt of foreign funding and its activities perceived as being against the national interest.

## Key Details
Greenpeace India was registered under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, and was required to file annual returns with the Ministry of Home Affairs, detailing its foreign contributions and their utilization. The organization received foreign funding from various sources, including Greenpeace International, and was required to comply with the provisions of the Act, including obtaining prior permission from the Central Government for the acceptance of foreign hospitality. The Act also requires organizations to maintain a separate account for foreign contributions and to utilize these contributions only for the purposes specified in the Act.

## Who Needs This
The Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, applies to all associations, companies, and individuals who receive foreign contributions, including non-governmental organizations (NGOs) like Greenpeace India. NGOs that receive foreign funding are required to register under the Act and comply with its provisions, including filing annual returns and maintaining separate accounts for foreign contributions. Companies and individuals who receive foreign hospitality are also required to comply with the Act and obtain prior permission from the Central Government.

## Step-by-Step Process
To comply with the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, NGOs like Greenpeace India are required to follow a step-by-step process, including:
* Registration under the Act
* Obtaining prior permission from the Central Government for the acceptance of foreign contributions
* Maintaining a separate account for foreign contributions
* Filing annual returns with the Ministry of Home Affairs
* Utilizing foreign contributions only for the purposes specified in the Act

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
NGOs like Greenpeace India are required to file their annual returns with the Ministry of Home Affairs by December 31st of each year. Failure to comply with the provisions of the Act can result in penalties, including the cancellation of registration, imposition of fines, and even prosecution. The Act also provides for the suspension of foreign contributions in cases where the organization is found to be in violation of the Act.
