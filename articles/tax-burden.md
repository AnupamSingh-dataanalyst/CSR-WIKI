# Tax burden
The tax burden refers to the total amount of taxes paid by an individual or organization, including direct and indirect taxes. In India, the tax burden is a significant concern for non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and companies, as it affects their financial sustainability and ability to achieve their objectives. Understanding the tax burden is essential for NGOs and companies to manage their finances effectively and comply with the relevant tax laws and regulations.

## Key Details
The Income-tax Act, 1961, and the Income Tax Rules, 1962, govern the tax burden in India. The tax burden includes income tax, which is levied on the taxable income of individuals and organizations. The taxable income is calculated by deducting the allowed deductions and exemptions from the gross income. The income tax rates in India range from 5% to 30% for individuals, and 25% to 30% for companies, depending on the taxable income. NGOs and companies must file their income tax returns using Form ITR-6 or Form ITR-7, as applicable, on the Income Tax Department's portal.

## Who Needs This
The tax burden applies to all NGOs and companies in India that have a taxable income. This includes NGOs registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, or the Companies Act, 2013. Companies, including private limited companies, public limited companies, and limited liability partnerships (LLPs), must also comply with the tax burden requirements. Additionally, foreign companies operating in India must also pay taxes on their Indian income.

## Step-by-Step Process
To calculate the tax burden, NGOs and companies must follow these steps:
1. Calculate the gross income from all sources, including donations, grants, and business income.
2. Deduct the allowed deductions and exemptions, such as donations to other NGOs, to calculate the taxable income.
3. Apply the relevant income tax rates to the taxable income to calculate the tax liability.
4. File the income tax return using Form ITR-6 or Form ITR-7, as applicable, on the Income Tax Department's portal.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadline for filing the income tax return is July 31st of each year, unless extended by the Income Tax Department. Late filing of the income tax return may attract a penalty of up to Rs. 10,000. Additionally, failure to pay the tax liability may attract interest and penalties, including a penalty of up to 100% of the tax liability.
