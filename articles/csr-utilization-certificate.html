CSR Utilization Certificate

A CSR Utilization Certificate is a document that certifies the proper use of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) funds by an implementing NGO. This certificate is essential for companies to ensure that their CSR expenditures are being utilized effectively and for the intended purposes. The certificate is typically issued by the implementing NGO or agency, confirming that the funds have been used in accordance with the approved CSR project.

Key Details

The CSR Utilization Certificate is a critical document that must be obtained by companies to comply with the CSR provisions under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013. The certificate should be issued on the company's letterhead, signed by the authorized representative of the implementing NGO, and should include details such as the amount of CSR funds utilized, the purpose of the project, and the duration of the project. The certificate should be submitted to the company along with the CSR project completion report, which includes the Form CSR-1 and Form CSR-2, as prescribed under the Companies (Corporate Social Responsibility Policy) Rules, 2014.

Who Needs This

The CSR Utilization Certificate is required by companies that have a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, as per Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013. These companies are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities. The implementing NGOs or agencies that receive CSR funds from these companies are also required to obtain the CSR Utilization Certificate to confirm that the funds have been utilized for the intended purposes.

Step-by-Step Process

To obtain a CSR Utilization Certificate, the implementing NGO or agency should follow these steps: - Ensure that the CSR project is completed and the funds have been utilized for the intended purposes. - Prepare a project completion report, including the Form CSR-1 and Form CSR-2. - Obtain a certificate from the company's auditor or chartered accountant, confirming that the CSR funds have been utilized in accordance with the approved project. - Issue the CSR Utilization Certificate on the company's letterhead, signed by the authorized representative of the implementing NGO.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The CSR Utilization Certificate should be obtained by the company within the specified timeframe, typically within 6 months from the end of the financial year in which the CSR expenditure was incurred. Failure to obtain the certificate or non-compliance with the CSR provisions may result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore and imprisonment of up to 3 years for the company's officers.
