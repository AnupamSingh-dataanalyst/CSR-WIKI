Women Empowerment CSR

Women Empowerment CSR refers to the corporate social responsibility initiatives undertaken by companies in India to promote the economic and social empowerment of women. These initiatives can include activities such as skill training, education, and healthcare, as well as support for self-help groups (SHGs) and other community-based organizations. The goal of Women Empowerment CSR is to promote gender equality and improve the overall well-being of women in India.

Key Details

The Companies Act, 2013, mandates that companies with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, spend at least 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities, including Women Empowerment CSR. Companies can undertake Women Empowerment CSR activities through their own foundations or by partnering with NGOs registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, or societies registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860. The Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) has also launched the CSR portal, where companies can report their CSR spending, including Women Empowerment CSR activities, using Form CSR-1.

Who Needs This

Women Empowerment CSR applies to companies that meet the threshold criteria under the Companies Act, 2013, as well as to NGOs and other organizations that partner with these companies to undertake Women Empowerment CSR activities. This includes companies in the private and public sectors, as well as foreign companies with operations in India. NGOs that work on women's empowerment issues, such as those focused on education, healthcare, and economic empowerment, can also benefit from partnering with companies on Women Empowerment CSR initiatives.

Step-by-Step Process

To undertake Women Empowerment CSR activities, companies can follow these steps: 1. Identify the Women Empowerment CSR activities they want to undertake, such as skill training or support for SHGs. 2. Partner with an NGO or other organization that has experience in implementing Women Empowerment CSR activities. 3. Develop a project proposal and budget for the Women Empowerment CSR activity. 4. Obtain approval from the company's CSR committee or board of directors. 5. Implement the Women Empowerment CSR activity and monitor its progress. 6. Report the CSR spending, including Women Empowerment CSR activities, on the CSR portal using Form CSR-1.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies that fail to spend the required 2% of their average net profit on CSR activities, including Women Empowerment CSR, can face penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 25 lakh and imprisonment of up to three years for the company's directors. Companies must also report their CSR spending, including Women Empowerment CSR activities, on the CSR portal within 30 days of the end of the financial year.
