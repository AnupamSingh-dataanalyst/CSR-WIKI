Taxation as Theft

Taxation as theft is a philosophical concept that views taxation as a form of coercion, where the government takes property from individuals without their consent. This idea is often discussed in the context of libertarianism and limited government intervention. In India, the concept of taxation as theft is not directly relevant to the country's tax laws, which are governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and other related regulations.

Key Details

The Income-tax Act, 1961, is the primary law governing income tax in India, and it defines the scope of taxable income, tax rates, and exemptions. The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) is responsible for administering the income-tax law, and it issues notifications, circulars, and guidelines to clarify the law. The income tax slabs and rates are revised annually through the Finance Act, which is passed by the Parliament of India. For example, for the assessment year 2023-24, the basic exemption limit for individuals is Rs. 2.5 lakhs, and the tax rates range from 5% to 30%.
