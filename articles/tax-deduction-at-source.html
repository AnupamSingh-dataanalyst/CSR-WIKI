Tax Deduction at Source

Tax deduction at source, commonly referred to as TDS, is a mechanism in India where a person responsible for making payments to another party is required to deduct a certain percentage of the payment as tax and deposit it with the government. This system is designed to collect tax at the source of income, ensuring that tax revenues are collected promptly and reducing the likelihood of tax evasion. The Income-tax Act, 1961, and subsequent amendments govern the provisions related to TDS.

Key Details

TDS is applicable on various types of payments, including salaries, interest, dividends, rent, and professional fees, among others. The threshold limits for TDS vary depending on the type of payment, with amounts below these limits not being subject to TDS. For instance, TDS on interest paid by banks is applicable if the interest exceeds Rs. 10,000 in a financial year. The deductor is required to file Form 26Q (for TDS on salaries) or Form 27Q (for TDS on other payments) quarterly, providing details of the tax deducted.

Who Needs This

TDS is applicable to all entities making payments that exceed the specified threshold limits, including companies, partnerships, and individuals. NGOs, trusts, and societies that make payments to employees, contractors, or vendors may also need to comply with TDS regulations. Any person responsible for making payments that are subject to TDS must obtain a Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number (TAN) from the Income Tax Department.

Step-by-Step Process

To comply with TDS regulations, the deductor must: (1) obtain a TAN, (2) calculate the tax deductible on each payment, (3) deduct the tax at the time of making the payment, (4) deposit the tax with the government within the specified due dates, and (5) file the quarterly TDS returns (Form 26Q or Form 27Q) with the Income Tax Department.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The due dates for depositing TDS with the government are the 7th of the next month for payments made between the 1st and 30th/31st of the previous month. Failure to deposit TDS on time may result in interest and penalties, including a late payment fee of 1.5% per month or part thereof. Additionally, the Income Tax Department may impose a penalty of up to Rs. 1 lakh for failure to file TDS returns on time.
