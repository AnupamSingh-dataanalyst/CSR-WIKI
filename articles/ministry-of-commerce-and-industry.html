Ministry of Commerce and Industry

The Ministry of Commerce and Industry is a government ministry in India that is responsible for the formulation of policies related to trade, commerce, and industry. It plays a crucial role in promoting economic growth and development in the country. The ministry is divided into two departments: the Department of Commerce and the Department of Industrial Policy and Promotion.

Key Details

The Ministry of Commerce and Industry is headed by a Minister of Commerce and Industry, who is assisted by a Minister of State. The ministry has a total of 27 divisions, including the Trade Policy Division, the Foreign Trade Division, and the State Trading Division. The ministry is also responsible for administering several key laws, including the Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992, and the Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951. Key forms and portals used by the ministry include the Importer Exporter Code (IEC) form, the Advanced Authorization Scheme, and the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) portal.

Who Needs This

The Ministry of Commerce and Industry is relevant to a wide range of organizations, including NGOs, companies, and individuals involved in trade, commerce, and industry. This includes exporters and importers, manufacturers, and service providers. In particular, NGOs and companies involved in international trade, e-commerce, and foreign investment will need to interact with the ministry and comply with its regulations.

Step-by-Step Process

To interact with the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, organizations will typically need to follow a series of steps, including: registering for an IEC code, obtaining necessary licenses and permits, and filing required forms and returns. For example, to obtain an IEC code, an organization will need to submit an online application through the DGFT portal, providing required documents such as a PAN card, a bank account statement, and a copy of the organization's incorporation certificate.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The Ministry of Commerce and Industry has several important deadlines and penalties that organizations need to be aware of. For example, the deadline for filing annual returns under the Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992 is September 30th of each year. Failure to comply with regulations can result in penalties, including fines and imprisonment. For example, under the Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, companies that fail to comply with regulations can be fined up to Rs. 10,000.
