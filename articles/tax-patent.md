# Tax patent
A tax patent in India refers to the authority granted to the central government to collect taxes on non-agricultural income, as per Entry 82 of the Union List of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India. This concept is crucial in understanding the income tax laws and regulations in the country. The income-tax law in India is governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and other related rules, notifications, and circulars.

## Key Details
The Income-tax Act, 1961, defines the scope of taxable income, including exemptions and deductions, while the Income Tax Rules, 1962, provide the procedures for tax assessment, payment, and refund. The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) issues notifications and circulars to clarify and implement the tax laws. The annual Finance Acts also introduce changes to the tax laws, including new provisions, amendments, and rates. For instance, the tax exemption limit for individuals is currently set at ₹2.5 lakhs, as per the Finance Act, 2020.

## Who Needs This
NGOs and companies in India that have a taxable income need to understand the concept of tax patent and comply with the income tax laws. This includes companies registered under the Companies Act, 2013, and NGOs registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, or the Indian Trusts Act, 1882. Specifically, NGOs that receive foreign contributions need to comply with the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, and file their tax returns using Form 10B.

## Step-by-Step Process
To obtain a tax exemption, NGOs need to register with the Income Tax Department and obtain a unique registration number. They must then file their tax returns using the prescribed forms, such as Form 10B, and claim exemptions under Section 11 of the Income-tax Act, 1961. The process involves:
* Registering with the Income Tax Department
* Obtaining a unique registration number
* Filing tax returns using the prescribed forms
* Claiming exemptions under Section 11 of the Income-tax Act, 1961

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The deadline for filing tax returns in India is typically July 31st of each year, although this may be extended in certain cases. Failure to file tax returns or pay taxes on time can result in penalties, including a fine of up to ₹10,000, as per Section 234F of the Income-tax Act, 1961.
