CSR Box Platform

The CSR Box Platform is an online platform designed to connect Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) with Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) donors, facilitating the implementation of CSR projects and activities. This platform aims to bridge the gap between NGOs and companies, enabling them to collaborate and work together towards achieving social and environmental goals. By leveraging technology, the CSR Box Platform streamlines the process of identifying, funding, and monitoring CSR initiatives.

Key Details

The CSR Box Platform is a digital marketplace that allows NGOs to showcase their projects and companies to find and fund suitable initiatives. To be eligible, NGOs must be registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, or Section 12A of the Income-tax Act, 1961, and have a valid FCRA registration if they receive foreign funding. Companies, on the other hand, must have a net worth of at least ₹500 crores or a turnover of ₹1000 crores to be eligible for CSR spending under the Companies Act, 2013.

Who Needs This

The CSR Box Platform is designed for NGOs and companies that want to engage in CSR activities. Specifically, it applies to NGOs that are looking for funding and support for their social and environmental projects, and companies that want to fulfill their CSR obligations under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013. This includes companies with a net worth of ₹500 crores or more, or a turnover of ₹1000 crores or more, that are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities.

Step-by-Step Process

To use the CSR Box Platform, NGOs and companies can follow these steps: 1. Registration: NGOs and companies must register on the platform by providing required documents and information. 2. Project Listing: NGOs can list their projects on the platform, providing details about the project, its objectives, and the funding required. 3. Project Selection: Companies can browse through the listed projects and select the ones that align with their CSR goals and objectives. 4. Funding and Implementation: Once a project is selected, the company can fund the project, and the NGO can implement it.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities every year. If a company fails to spend the required amount, it must disclose the reasons for not spending in its annual report. Additionally, the company must transfer any unspent amount to a CSR fund or a registered trust within six months of the end of the financial year.
