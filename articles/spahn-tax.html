Spahn tax

The Spahn tax, also known as the tax on income from foreign sources, is a concept in Indian income tax law that deals with the taxation of foreign income earned by Indian residents. This tax is relevant for individuals and organizations, including NGOs and companies, that have international operations or receive income from foreign sources. The Spahn tax is governed by the provisions of the Income-tax Act, 1961, and is subject to the rules and regulations specified by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT).

Key Details

The Spahn tax is calculated on the income earned from foreign sources, which includes income from foreign business, profession, or vocation, as well as income from foreign investments, such as dividends, interest, and royalties. The tax rate for Spahn tax is the same as the tax rate applicable to the individual or organization's domestic income, which ranges from 10% to 30% for individuals and 25% to 30% for companies. The tax is required to be paid on the income earned during the financial year, which runs from April 1 to March 31, and the return of income is required to be filed by July 31 of the assessment year.

Who Needs This

The Spahn tax applies to all Indian residents, including individuals, companies, and NGOs, that earn income from foreign sources. This includes income earned from foreign subsidiaries, joint ventures, or associate companies, as well as income earned from foreign clients or customers. NGOs that receive foreign donations or grants are also required to pay Spahn tax on such income, unless exempt under Section 10(23C) or Section 11 of the Income-tax Act, 1961.

Step-by-Step Process

To pay the Spahn tax, the taxpayer is required to follow these steps: 1. Calculate the income earned from foreign sources during the financial year. 2. Calculate the tax liability on such income at the applicable tax rate. 3. Pay the tax liability through the online portal of the Income Tax Department, using Form 280 or Form 281, as applicable. 4. File the return of income, including the foreign income, by July 31 of the assessment year, using Form ITR-2 or Form ITR-3, as applicable.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The deadline for paying the Spahn tax is the same as the deadline for filing the return of income, which is July 31 of the assessment year. Failure to pay the tax or file the return on time can result in penalties, including a late fee of Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 10,000, as well as interest on the tax liability at the rate of 1% to 2% per month.
