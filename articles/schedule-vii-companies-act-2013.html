Schedule VII Companies Act 2013

The Schedule VII of the Companies Act 2013 outlines the activities that are eligible for Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) spending in India. This schedule is crucial for companies and NGOs to understand the permissible areas of CSR expenditure, ensuring that their social responsibility initiatives are aligned with the regulatory framework. By specifying the eligible activities, Schedule VII provides a clear guideline for companies to undertake CSR initiatives that contribute to the social and economic development of the country.

Key Details

Schedule VII lists a range of activities that are eligible for CSR spending, including: * Eradicating hunger, poverty, and malnutrition * Promoting education, employability, and vocational skills * Promoting gender equality, empowering women, and setting up homes and hostels for women and orphans * Ensuring environmental sustainability, ecological balance, conservation of natural resources, and protecting flora and fauna * Protection of national heritage, art, and culture * Measures for the benefit of armed forces veterans, war widows, and their dependents * Training to promote rural sports, nationally recognized sports, and Olympic sports * Contribution to the Prime Minister's National Relief Fund or any other fund set up by the Central Government for socio-economic development Companies are required to spend at least 2% of their average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities specified in Schedule VII.

Who Needs This

This schedule applies to companies that have a net worth of Rs. 500 crores or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1000 crores or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crores or more during any financial year. Such companies are required to constitute a CSR Committee and formulate a CSR policy that outlines their CSR initiatives and expenditures. NGOs and other organizations that partner with companies for CSR initiatives also need to be aware of the activities specified in Schedule VII to ensure that their projects are eligible for CSR funding.

Step-by-Step Process

While there is no specific step-by-step process for Schedule VII, companies are required to follow these general steps: * Calculate their CSR expenditure based on their average net profits * Formulate a CSR policy and constitute a CSR Committee * Identify eligible CSR activities from Schedule VII * Implement CSR projects and maintain records of expenditures * File the CSR report in Form CSR-2 with the Registrar of Companies

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies are required to spend the specified amount on CSR activities during each financial year. If a company fails to spend the required amount, it must disclose the reasons for not spending in its Board's report. The company is also required to transfer any unspent amount to a CSR fund or a specified fund within six months of the end of the financial year. Failure to comply with CSR regulations may result in penalties, including fines and imprisonment.
