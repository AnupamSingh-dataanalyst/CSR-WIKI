Akanksha Foundation

The Akanksha Foundation is a non-profit organization in India that aims to provide high-quality education to disadvantaged children. Founded in 1991 by Shaheen Mistri, the foundation works to bridge the gap in education between privileged and underprivileged children. With a focus on creating a holistic learning environment, Akanksha Foundation operates schools and programs in Mumbai and Pune, impacting the lives of thousands of children.

Key Details

The Akanksha Foundation has a strong focus on teacher training and community engagement, with over 500 staff members and 7,500 students across its centers. The foundation's programs include the Akanksha School Project, the Centre of Excellence, and the Young Inspirators Programme, among others. Akanksha Foundation is registered under the Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, and is eligible to receive foreign contributions under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010.

Who Needs This

The Akanksha Foundation's programs and services are primarily aimed at underprivileged children in Mumbai and Pune, with a focus on those living in slum areas. NGOs and companies working in the education sector, particularly those focused on disadvantaged communities, may find it beneficial to collaborate with or support the Akanksha Foundation. Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives of companies may also consider partnering with Akanksha Foundation to contribute to the education and development of underprivileged children.
