Income Tax Act

The Income Tax Act is a comprehensive legislation in India that governs the taxation of income earned by individuals, companies, and other entities. It outlines the rules and regulations for assessing, levying, and collecting income tax, and provides exemptions, deductions, and reliefs to eligible taxpayers. The Act is administered by the Income Tax Department, which is responsible for ensuring compliance with its provisions.

Key Details

The Income Tax Act, 1961, is the primary legislation governing income tax in India. Key details include the different types of income that are taxable, such as salary, business income, capital gains, and income from other sources. The Act also specifies the tax rates, slabs, and ceilings, such as the basic exemption limit of ₹2.5 lakhs for individuals, and the various forms that need to be filed, including Form 16, Form 16A, and Form ITR-1 to Form ITR-7. Additionally, the Act provides for tax deductions and exemptions under sections 80C, 80D, and 80G, among others.

Who Needs This

The Income Tax Act applies to all individuals, companies, and other entities that earn income in India, including NGOs, trusts, and societies. NGOs registered under the Income Tax Act, 1961, are required to file their income tax returns and claim exemptions under Section 11 and Section 12 of the Act. Companies, including those engaged in corporate social responsibility (CSR) activities, must also comply with the provisions of the Act. Furthermore, foreign companies and individuals with a presence in India are also subject to the provisions of the Act.

Step-by-Step Process

To comply with the Income Tax Act, taxpayers need to follow a step-by-step process, which includes: (1) obtaining a Permanent Account Number (PAN), (2) filing their income tax returns using the appropriate form, (3) paying any taxes due, and (4) claiming refunds, if eligible. NGOs and companies must also maintain accurate records and accounts, and ensure that their financial statements are audited and filed with the Income Tax Department.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The Income Tax Act specifies important deadlines for filing income tax returns, paying taxes, and claiming refunds. The deadline for filing individual tax returns is typically July 31st of each year, while companies and NGOs must file their returns by September 30th. Failure to comply with these deadlines can result in penalties, interest, and other consequences, including a late fee of ₹5,000, and interest at the rate of 1% per month or part thereof.
