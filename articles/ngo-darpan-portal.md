# NGO Darpan Portal
The NGO Darpan Portal is an online platform launched by NITI Aayog to facilitate the registration of non-governmental organizations (NGOs) in India, aiming to bring transparency and accountability in the sector. This portal allows NGOs to register themselves and provides access to information on government grants and schemes. By registering on the portal, NGOs can also apply for grants and funding from various government ministries and departments.

## Key Details
The NGO Darpan Portal was launched in 2016, and as of now, it has more than 80,000 registered NGOs. To register on the portal, NGOs need to provide basic information such as their name, address, objectives, and details of their governing body. They also need to upload documents like their registration certificate, PAN card, and audited financial statements. The registration process is free, and NGOs can register online by filling up the registration form on the portal.

## Who Needs This
The NGO Darpan Portal is applicable to all NGOs operating in India, including trusts, societies, and section 8 companies. These NGOs can register on the portal to access information on government grants and schemes, and to apply for funding. Additionally, companies operating in India that want to undertake corporate social responsibility (CSR) activities can also use the portal to find and partner with registered NGOs.

## Step-by-Step Process
To register on the NGO Darpan Portal, NGOs need to follow these steps:
1. Visit the NGO Darpan Portal website and click on the "Register" button.
2. Fill up the registration form with basic information such as name, address, objectives, and details of the governing body.
3. Upload required documents such as registration certificate, PAN card, and audited financial statements.
4. Verify the email address and mobile number.
5. Submit the registration form and wait for the approval.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
There are no specific deadlines for registering on the NGO Darpan Portal, and NGOs can register at any time. However, NGOs that are already registered under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, need to renew their registration every five years. Failure to renew the registration can result in penalties and cancellation of the registration.
