Section 135 Companies Act 2013

Section 135 of the Companies Act 2013 is a landmark provision that mandates certain companies in India to spend a portion of their profits on corporate social responsibility (CSR) activities. This provision aims to promote social and economic development by encouraging companies to contribute to the well-being of society. By making CSR spending mandatory, the government seeks to ensure that businesses give back to the community and help address social and environmental challenges.

Key Details

The key details of Section 135 are as follows: every company with a net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more, or a turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more, or a net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more, must spend at least 2% of its average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities. The CSR expenditure should be made in accordance with the CSR policy of the company, which must be approved by the company's board of directors. Companies must also disclose their CSR policy and spending in their annual reports, using Form CSR-2 for the CSR report.

Who Needs This

This provision applies to all companies incorporated in India, including private and public companies, that meet the specified thresholds. This includes companies in various sectors, such as manufacturing, services, and infrastructure. NGOs and other organizations that partner with companies for CSR activities must also be aware of the provisions of Section 135. Additionally, companies must ensure that their CSR activities are aligned with the Schedule VII of the Companies Act 2013, which lists out the eligible CSR activities.

Step-by-Step Process

To comply with Section 135, companies must follow these steps: 1. Calculate their average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years. 2. Determine the amount to be spent on CSR activities, which is at least 2% of the average net profits. 3. Formulate a CSR policy, which must be approved by the board of directors. 4. Identify and select CSR projects and activities that align with the company's CSR policy and Schedule VII of the Companies Act 2013. 5. Spend the specified amount on CSR activities during the financial year. 6. Disclose the CSR policy and spending in the company's annual report, using Form CSR-2.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies must spend the specified amount on CSR activities during the financial year, and any unspent amount must be transferred to a CSR fund or a registered trust. If a company fails to spend the required amount, it must provide reasons for the same in its annual report. The company may also be liable to pay a penalty, which can range from Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 25 lakh, and every officer of the company who is in default may be liable to pay a penalty, which can range from Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakh.
