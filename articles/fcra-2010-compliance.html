FCRA 2010 Compliance

The Foreign Contribution Regulation Act (FCRA) 2010 is a law that regulates the acceptance and utilization of foreign contributions by NGOs and other entities in India. To ensure transparency and accountability, the FCRA mandates annual compliance requirements for registered entities. These requirements help the government monitor the inflow and utilization of foreign funds, ensuring they are used for legitimate purposes.

Key Details

The FCRA 2010 requires registered entities to file an annual return in Form FC-4, which includes details of foreign contributions received and utilized during the financial year. The return must be filed online through the FCRA online services portal, and a hard copy of the return along with supporting documents must be submitted to the Ministry of Home Affairs. The annual return must be filed within nine months of the end of the financial year, i.e., by December 31st of each year.

Who Needs This

FCRA 2010 compliance applies to all NGOs, associations, and entities registered under the FCRA, including those that have been granted prior permission to receive foreign contributions. This includes organizations working in various sectors such as education, healthcare, social welfare, and human rights. Companies that have been granted permission to receive foreign contributions under the FCRA also need to comply with these regulations.

Step-by-Step Process

To comply with FCRA 2010, registered entities must follow these steps: 1. Collect and compile all relevant documents, including bank statements, receipts, and utilization certificates. 2. File the annual return in Form FC-4 online through the FCRA online services portal. 3. Submit a hard copy of the return along with supporting documents to the Ministry of Home Affairs. 4. Ensure that all foreign contributions are accounted for and utilized in accordance with the objectives of the organization.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The annual return in Form FC-4 must be filed by December 31st of each year. Failure to file the return or submitting incomplete information can result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 25,000 or even cancellation of FCRA registration. It is essential to comply with the deadlines and requirements to avoid any penalties or adverse action.
