Environmental tax

Environmental tax, also known as green tax, is a type of tax levied on activities or products that harm the environment, such as pollution, deforestation, or carbon emissions. The primary goal of environmental tax is to discourage harmful environmental practices and encourage sustainable development. In India, environmental tax is governed by various laws and regulations, including the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, and the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974.

Key Details

The environmental tax in India is imposed on various activities, including the manufacture, sale, and use of certain products, such as plastics, pesticides, and fossil fuels. The tax rates vary depending on the type of activity or product, and are specified in the Finance Acts and Notifications issued by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT). For example, the Clean Energy Cess, which is a type of environmental tax, is levied on coal, lignite, and peat at a rate of Rs. 400 per tonne.

Who Needs This

Environmental tax applies to various entities, including companies, industries, and individuals, who engage in activities that harm the environment. This includes manufacturers, suppliers, and users of environmentally harmful products, as well as those who generate pollution or waste. Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) and other organizations that work on environmental issues may also need to be aware of environmental tax laws and regulations.

Step-by-Step Process

The process of paying environmental tax in India involves several steps, including registration, tax payment, and filing of returns. The specific steps vary depending on the type of tax and the entity liable to pay it. For example, companies that manufacture or sell environmentally harmful products may need to register with the Central Excise Department and obtain a license to operate. They may also need to file periodic returns and pay taxes on a quarterly or annual basis.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

The deadlines for paying environmental tax in India vary depending on the type of tax and the entity liable to pay it. For example, the deadline for paying the Clean Energy Cess is the 6th of each month. Failure to pay environmental tax on time can result in penalties, including fines and interest on the amount due. The penalties can range from 10% to 100% of the tax amount, depending on the severity of the offense.
