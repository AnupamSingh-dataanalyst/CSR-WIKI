Non-governmental organisations in India

Non-governmental organisations (NGOs) in India are entities that operate independently of the government to address various social, economic, and environmental issues. These organisations play a crucial role in promoting development, equality, and justice in the country. With over 3 million NGOs registered in India, they constitute a significant sector that contributes to the nation's growth and well-being.

Key Details

NGOs in India are registered under various laws, including the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, the Societies Registration Act, 1860, and the Companies Act, 2013. To register as an NGO, organisations must submit required documents, such as the Memorandum of Association, Articles of Association, and a certificate of incorporation, to the relevant authorities. Additionally, NGOs must file annual returns with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs through the MCA21 portal and obtain a unique ID, known as the Darpan ID, from the NITI Aayog.

Who Needs This

NGOs, charities, and non-profit organisations operating in India need to comply with the relevant laws and regulations. Companies that engage in corporate social responsibility (CSR) activities also need to partner with registered NGOs to claim tax exemptions under Section 80G of the Income-tax Act, 1961. Furthermore, foreign NGOs seeking to operate in India must register with the Ministry of Home Affairs under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010.

Step-by-Step Process

To register an NGO in India, the following steps can be taken: 1. Choose a name and ensure its availability. 2. Prepare the Memorandum and Articles of Association. 3. Register with the relevant authorities, such as the Registrar of Societies or the Registrar of Companies. 4. Obtain a PAN and TAN from the Income Tax Department. 5. Register with the NITI Aayog to obtain a Darpan ID.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

NGOs must file their annual returns within 6 months of the end of the financial year. Failure to do so may result in penalties, such as a fine of up to ₹1 lakh. Additionally, NGOs that receive foreign funding must file their returns with the Ministry of Home Affairs within 9 months of the end of the financial year.
