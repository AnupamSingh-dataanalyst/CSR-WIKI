# President of India
The President of India is the head of state and the supreme commander of the Indian Armed Forces. The President plays a crucial role in the governance of the country, including giving assent to bills passed by the Parliament, which makes them into laws. The President's assent is essential for any bill to become an act, as seen in the case of The Companies Act, 2013, which received presidential assent on 29 August 2013.

## Key Details
The President of India is elected by an electoral college consisting of the members of both houses of parliament and the legislative assemblies of the states. The President serves a term of five years and is eligible for re-election. The President's role is largely ceremonial, with most executive powers being exercised by the Prime Minister and the Council of Ministers.

## Who Needs This
All companies and NGOs in India are indirectly affected by the President's role, as the President gives assent to bills that become laws governing their operations. Specifically, companies governed by The Companies Act, 2013, need to be aware of the President's role in giving assent to amendments to the act. NGOs and companies that receive government funding or work with government agencies also need to understand the President's role in the governance of the country.

## Step-by-Step Process
There is no specific step-by-step process for interacting with the President of India, as the President's role is largely ceremonial and most interactions are with government ministries and agencies. However, companies and NGOs may need to follow certain procedures when dealing with government agencies or seeking funding, which may involve referencing laws and regulations that have received presidential assent.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
There are no specific deadlines or penalties related to the President of India, as the President's role is not directly involved in the day-to-day operations of companies and NGOs. However, companies and NGOs must comply with laws and regulations that have received presidential assent, and failure to do so may result in penalties and fines.
