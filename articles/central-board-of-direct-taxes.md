# Central Board of Direct Taxes
The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) is a governing body that oversees the administration of direct taxes in India, including income tax, wealth tax, and gift tax. It operates under the Department of Revenue, Ministry of Finance, and is responsible for framing policies and guidelines related to direct taxes. The CBDT plays a crucial role in ensuring that the income-tax law is implemented effectively and efficiently across the country.

## Key Details
The CBDT is headed by a Chairman and consists of six members, who are responsible for managing the direct tax system in India. The CBDT issues various forms, such as Form 16, Form 16A, and Form 24Q, which are used for filing income tax returns, claiming tax deductions, and reporting tax withheld at source. The CBDT also issues circulars, notifications, and guidelines, such as the Income Tax Rules 1962, to clarify the provisions of the Income-tax Act, 1961.

## Who Needs This
The CBDT's guidelines and policies apply to all individuals, companies, and organizations that are required to pay direct taxes in India, including NGOs, charities, and corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives. Specifically, this includes entities that have a taxable income above the threshold limit, which is currently Rs. 2.5 lakhs for individuals and Rs. 1 lakh for companies. NGOs and charities that are exempt from income tax under Section 11 of the Income-tax Act, 1961, are still required to file annual returns with the CBDT.

## Step-by-Step Process
To comply with the CBDT's guidelines, individuals and organizations need to follow a step-by-step process, which includes:
* Filing income tax returns in the prescribed forms, such as Form 16, Form 16A, and Form 24Q
* Paying advance tax, if applicable
* Claiming tax deductions and exemptions, if eligible
* Reporting tax withheld at source, if applicable
* Filing annual returns with the CBDT, if required

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The CBDT has specified deadlines for filing income tax returns, paying advance tax, and reporting tax withheld at source. Failure to comply with these deadlines can result in penalties, such as:
* Late filing fees, which can range from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000
* Interest on unpaid taxes, which can range from 6% to 12% per annum
* Penalty for non-compliance, which can range from Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 1 lakh
