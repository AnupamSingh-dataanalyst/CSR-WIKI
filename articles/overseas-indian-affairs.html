Overseas Indian Affairs

Overseas Indian Affairs refers to the government of India's initiatives and policies aimed at connecting with and supporting the Indian diaspora worldwide. The Ministry of External Affairs, through its Department of Overseas Indians, is responsible for promoting the interests of overseas Indians and providing them with necessary support and services. This includes facilitating their engagement with India, protecting their rights, and addressing their concerns.

Key Details

The government of India has introduced various schemes and initiatives to engage with overseas Indians, such as the Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) scheme, the Persons of Indian Origin (PIO) scheme, and the Know India Programme (KIP). The OCI scheme, for example, allows overseas Indians to acquire lifetime visa to visit India, own property, and undertake investments, among other benefits. To be eligible for the OCI scheme, an applicant must meet certain criteria, such as being a citizen of a country that allows dual citizenship or being a spouse of an OCI cardholder.

Who Needs This

Overseas Indian Affairs is relevant to Indian NGOs and companies that have international operations, partnerships, or fundraising activities involving overseas Indians. This includes NGOs that receive foreign contributions, companies that have overseas subsidiaries or affiliates, and individuals who are overseas citizens of India or persons of Indian origin. Additionally, companies that are required to comply with the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, and the Companies Act, 2013, may need to engage with the government's initiatives on overseas Indian affairs.

Step-by-Step Process

To engage with the government's initiatives on overseas Indian affairs, NGOs and companies can follow these steps: * Register with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs and obtain a registration number under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, if applicable. * Apply for an OCI card or a PIO card, if eligible. * Participate in government-sponsored programs and initiatives, such as the Know India Programme (KIP) or the Pravasi Bharatiya Divas (PBD) conference. * Network with overseas Indian communities and associations to promote business, cultural, and social ties.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

NGOs and companies that fail to comply with the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010, or the Companies Act, 2013, may face penalties, such as fines or imprisonment. The government may also impose restrictions on foreign funding or investments if the NGO or company is found to be non-compliant. It is essential to note that the deadlines for registration, reporting, and compliance vary depending on the specific regulations and laws applicable to the NGO or company.
