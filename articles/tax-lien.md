# Tax Lien
A tax lien is a legal claim filed by the Income Tax Department against a taxpayer's assets, including property and bank accounts, for unpaid taxes. This claim gives the government a priority right to the assets, allowing it to collect the outstanding tax amount. In India, tax liens are governed by the Income-tax Act, 1961, and are used as a measure to recover unpaid taxes from defaulting taxpayers.

## Key Details
The Income Tax Department can file a tax lien against a taxpayer who has outstanding tax dues exceeding Rs. 5 lakhs. The tax lien is filed in Form 26AS, which is a statement of tax credits, and is also reflected in the taxpayer's permanent account number (PAN) ledger account. The tax lien remains in force until the outstanding tax amount is paid in full, along with any applicable interest and penalties.

## Who Needs This
Tax liens are applicable to all taxpayers, including individuals, Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs), companies, and non-governmental organizations (NGOs), who have outstanding tax dues. NGOs and companies with tax-exempt status under Section 11 or Section 12A of the Income-tax Act, 1961, are also not exempt from tax liens if they have unpaid taxes.

## Step-by-Step Process
To remove a tax lien, the taxpayer must pay the outstanding tax amount, along with any applicable interest and penalties, to the Income Tax Department. The taxpayer can pay the tax amount online through the National Securities Depository Limited (NSDL) website or by submitting a cheque or demand draft at the designated bank branches. After paying the tax amount, the taxpayer must file Form 26QC, which is a statement of tax paid, to update their tax ledger account.

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
The taxpayer must pay the outstanding tax amount within the time limit specified in the tax demand notice, which is usually 30 days from the date of the notice. Failure to pay the tax amount within the specified time limit can result in additional penalties, including a penalty of up to 1% of the outstanding tax amount per month.
