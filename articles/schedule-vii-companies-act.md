# Schedule VII Companies Act
The Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013, outlines the activities that can be undertaken by companies to fulfill their Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) obligations. This provision is a significant aspect of the Act, as it encourages companies to contribute to social and environmental causes. By specifying the eligible activities, Schedule VII provides a framework for companies to engage in CSR initiatives.

## Key Details
Schedule VII lists out 10 broad categories of activities that companies can undertake to fulfill their CSR obligations, including:
* Eradicating hunger, poverty, and malnutrition
* Promoting education and vocational skills
* Promoting gender equality and empowering women
* Ensuring environmental sustainability
* Protection of national heritage, art, and culture
The CSR expenditure is calculated as 2% of the average net profits of the company made during the three immediately preceding financial years. Companies can also collaborate with registered trusts, societies, or Section 8 companies to implement their CSR projects.

## Who Needs This
This provision applies to companies that meet the following criteria:
* Net worth of Rs. 500 crore or more
* Turnover of Rs. 1,000 crore or more
* Net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more
All companies that meet these thresholds are required to constitute a CSR Committee and allocate 2% of their average net profits towards CSR activities.

## Step-by-Step Process
To comply with Schedule VII, companies should:
1. Calculate their average net profits for the three preceding financial years
2. Determine their CSR expenditure as 2% of the average net profits
3. Constitute a CSR Committee to oversee the implementation of CSR projects
4. Identify eligible CSR activities from the list specified in Schedule VII
5. Allocate the CSR budget to the selected projects

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
Companies are required to spend the allocated CSR amount within the financial year, and any unspent amount must be transferred to a separate bank account called the Unspent Corporate Social Responsibility Account. Failure to comply with Schedule VII may result in penalties, including a fine of up to Rs. 1 crore.
