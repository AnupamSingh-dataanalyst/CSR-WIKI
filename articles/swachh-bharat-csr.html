Swachh Bharat CSR

The Swachh Bharat CSR initiative is a part of the Indian government's broader Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Clean India Mission) aimed at improving sanitation and cleanliness across the country. Under this initiative, companies can undertake Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) projects focused on sanitation, hygiene, and waste management. By supporting such projects, companies can contribute to the national goal of a cleaner India while also fulfilling their CSR obligations under the Companies Act, 2013.

Key Details

The Swachh Bharat CSR initiative encourages companies to invest in projects such as construction of toilets, community sanitary complexes, and solid waste management facilities. Companies can spend their CSR funds on these projects as per Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013, which lists "contributions to the Prime Minister's National Relief Fund or any other fund set up by the Central Government for socio-economic development and relief and funds for the welfare of the Scheduled Castes, the Scheduled Tribes, other backward classes, minorities and women" and "such other matters as may be prescribed" - which includes sanitation and cleanliness. The Ministry of Corporate Affairs, through notifications and circulars, has clarified that Swachh Bharat Abhiyan is an eligible CSR activity. Companies can also collaborate with NGOs and government agencies to implement these projects.

Who Needs This

This initiative applies to all companies that come under the purview of Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, which mandates that every company having a net worth of rupees five hundred crores or more, or a turnover of rupees one thousand crores or more, or a net profit of rupees five crores or more during any financial year, shall constitute a CSR committee and spend at least 2% of the average net profits of the company made during the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities. NGOs working in the area of sanitation and cleanliness can also benefit from this initiative by partnering with companies for CSR projects.

Step-by-Step Process

To undertake a Swachh Bharat CSR project, a company should first identify the specific project it wishes to support, which could range from constructing toilets in schools to implementing solid waste management systems in urban or rural areas. The company should then ensure that the project aligns with the Schedule VII activities under the Companies Act, 2013. Next, the company should select an implementing partner, which could be an NGO, a government agency, or another eligible entity. The company must then approve the project and allocate the necessary CSR funds. Implementation and monitoring of the project should be done in accordance with the company's CSR policy and applicable laws.

Important Deadlines / Penalties

Companies are required to spend the mandated 2% of their average net profits on CSR activities within the financial year. If a company fails to spend the required amount, it must disclose the reasons for not spending in its Board's report. The company is also required to transfer any unspent CSR amount to a fund specified by the Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013, within six months of the end of the financial year. Non-compliance with CSR provisions can lead to penalties as per the Companies Act, 2013.
