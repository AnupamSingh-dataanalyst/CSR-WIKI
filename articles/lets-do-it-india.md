# Let's Do It! India
Let's Do It! India is a national campaign that aims to clean up India's waste and promote a cleaner environment. The campaign is part of the global Let's Do It! movement, which has inspired millions of people worldwide to take action against waste and pollution. The Indian chapter of the campaign focuses on mobilizing citizens, NGOs, and corporations to work together towards a cleaner and more sustainable future.

## Key Details
Let's Do It! India is a collaborative effort between various stakeholders, including the government, NGOs, and private companies. The campaign has set specific goals, such as cleaning up 10 million tons of waste and engaging 10 million volunteers across the country. The initiative uses digital platforms, including the Let's Do It! India website and mobile app, to mobilize volunteers, track progress, and provide resources and support to participants.

## Who Needs This
Let's Do It! India is relevant to various organizations and individuals, including NGOs, community groups, schools, and corporations. Any organization or individual interested in contributing to a cleaner environment and promoting sustainability can participate in the campaign. Companies can also use the campaign as an opportunity to fulfill their Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) obligations under the Companies Act, 2013, and contribute to the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan mission.

## Step-by-Step Process
To participate in Let's Do It! India, organizations and individuals can follow these steps:
* Register on the Let's Do It! India website or mobile app
* Choose a location and date for the cleanup activity
* Mobilize volunteers and resources
* Conduct the cleanup activity and report progress on the platform
* Share experiences and photos on social media using the campaign's hashtags

## Important Deadlines / Penalties
While there are no specific deadlines or penalties for participating in Let's Do It! India, organizations and individuals are encouraged to plan and execute their cleanup activities in a timely and responsible manner. Companies participating in the campaign as part of their CSR initiatives must ensure compliance with the CSR reporting requirements under the Companies Act, 2013, and file their CSR reports in Form MGT-14 within the prescribed timeframe.
